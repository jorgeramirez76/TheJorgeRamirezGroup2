Basking Ridge, NJ Real Estate Market Report — March 2026

By Jorge Ramirez | March 2026

Out of curiosity, have you been following the real estate market in Basking Ridge, NJ? Whether you're thinking about buying, selling, or just keeping tabs on your home's value, this report gives you the data you need to make smart decisions.

Market Snapshot — Basking Ridge NJ (March 2026)

  • Median Home Price: $720K
  • Year-Over-Year Change: +4.6%
  • Average Days on Market: 18–25 days
  • Market Type: Seller's market
  • County: Somerset County

What's Driving the Basking Ridge Market?

Basking Ridge is Somerset County's crown jewel—award-winning schools, a historic town center, and a community centered around the famous 600-year-old White Oak tree. The commute advantage (NJ Transit Raritan Valley Line ~55 minutes to NYC) continues to be a major draw for NYC-area professionals, particularly as hybrid work schedules make in-office days fewer but commute quality more important than ever.

The Bernards Township School District (consistently top-rated in Somerset County) remains a powerful demand driver — families consistently prioritize this district when relocating to the area, creating a floor of steady buyer demand regardless of broader market conditions.

Inventory & Competition

Inventory in Basking Ridge remains constrained, keeping buyers in competition for quality listings. The neighborhoods most in demand include The Ridge community, downtown Basking Ridge, and the Liberty Corner section.

Homes priced correctly and presented well are still seeing multiple offers in the first week. Buyers should be pre-approved and ready to move.

Price Trends

With +4.6% year-over-year appreciation, Basking Ridge continues to build equity for homeowners. This growth reflects the town's desirability, limited housing stock, and strong commuter appeal. Looking ahead to the second half of 2026, I expect prices to remain firm, with modest continued appreciation in the 3–5% range.

Advice for Buyers

What's been the biggest challenge with finding a home in Basking Ridge lately? Here are some tips:

  • Get pre-approved before touring — a conditional offer is less competitive in this market
  • Focus on neighborhoods near The Ridge community for best resale value
  • Budget carefully for NJ property taxes — they're significant in Basking Ridge
  • Don't skip the home inspection even under offer pressure

Advice for Sellers

Help me understand—what have you been considering when thinking about selling your home in Basking Ridge? Here are some suggestions:

  • Price based on recent comparable sales — not wishful thinking or Zillow estimates
  • Invest in professional photography; 95% of buyers start their search online
  • Spring (March–June) remains the peak window for maximum buyer exposure
  • Consider pre-listing inspections to remove buyer objections upfront
