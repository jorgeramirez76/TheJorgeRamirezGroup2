The Chatham Market at a Glance (2026)

As of early 2026, the Chatham real estate market is a seller's market with homes spending an average of 18–25 days on the market. Neighborhoods to Know

Chatham offers several distinct neighborhoods, including Chatham Borough (walkable, near downtown) and Chatham Township (larger lots, more private). Whether you're looking for walkability, larger lots, or proximity to the train, Chatham has options at different price points. I'll help you identify which neighborhood best matches your lifestyle and budget.

Schools

One of the biggest draws to Chatham is the Chatham School District (consistently top 5 in NJ). The district consistently earns strong ratings for academic achievement, teacher quality, and extracurricular opportunities. For families, school district boundaries can significantly affect home values — I'll make sure you understand which school your prospective home falls into.

Commute to NYC

For NYC commuters, Chatham's location is a key advantage. The typical commute is via NJ Transit Morris & Essex Line ~55 minutes to NYC. With hybrid and remote work arrangements common, many buyers today can absorb a slightly longer commute in exchange for Chatham's quality of life and home values.

What Buyers Should Know in 2026

Here's what I tell every buyer I work with in Chatham:

  1. Get pre-approved before you look — in a seller's market, sellers won't take an offer seriously without it.
  2. Know your neighborhood priorities — commute distance, school zone, lot size, and walkability all vary by block.
  3. Be ready to move fast — desirable homes in Chatham often receive offers within the first weekend. Have your documents ready.
  4. Budget for property taxes — New Jersey's property taxes are significant. Factor the full PITI payment into your budget.
  5. Don't skip the inspection — many Chatham homes are 50–80 years old; a thorough inspection protects you.
