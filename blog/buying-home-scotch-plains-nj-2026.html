Schools

One of the biggest draws to Scotch Plains is the Scotch Plains-Fanwood School District. The district consistently earns strong ratings for academic achievement, teacher quality, and extracurricular opportunities. For families, school district boundaries can significantly affect home values — I'll make sure you understand which school your prospective home falls into.

Commute to NYC

For NYC commuters, Scotch Plains's location is a key advantage. The typical commute is via NJ Transit Raritan Valley Line ~50 minutes to NYC. With hybrid and remote work arrangements common, many buyers today can absorb a slightly longer commute in exchange for Scotch Plains's quality of life and home values.

What Buyers Should Know in 2026

Here's what I tell every buyer I work with in Scotch Plains:

  1. Get pre-approved before you look — in a seller's market, sellers won't take an offer seriously without it.
  2. Know your neighborhood priorities — commute distance, school zone, lot size, and walkability all vary by block.
  3. Be ready to move fast — desirable homes in Scotch Plains often receive offers within the first weekend. Have your documents ready.
  4. Budget for property taxes — New Jersey's property taxes are significant. Factor the full PITI payment into your budget.
  5. Don't skip the inspection — many Scotch Plains homes are 50–80 years old; a thorough inspection protects you.
