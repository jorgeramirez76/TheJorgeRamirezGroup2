The South Orange Market at a Glance (2026)

As of early 2026, the South Orange real estate market is a seller's market with homes spending an average of 22–30 days on the market. Neighborhoods to Know

South Orange offers several distinct neighborhoods, each with its own unique character and amenities. From the SOMA arts district to Newstead and Flood's Hill, there are options to suit every lifestyle and budget. It can be overwhelming to navigate, but I can help you identify which neighborhood aligns best with your needs.

Schools

One of the biggest draws to South Orange is the South Orange-Maplewood School District (Columbia High School). The district consistently earns strong ratings for academic achievement, teacher quality, and extracurricular opportunities. For families, school district boundaries can significantly affect home values, and I can ensure you understand which school your prospective home falls into.

Commute to NYC

For those who value proximity to the city, South Orange’s location is a key advantage. The typical commute is via NJ Transit Midtown Direct — just 27 minutes to NYC Penn Station! With hybrid and remote work arrangements now common, many buyers today can balance a slightly longer commute with the quality of life and home values South Orange offers.

What Buyers Should Know in 2026

Here’s what I often hear from buyers considering South Orange:

  1. Get pre-approved before you look — in a seller's market, sellers won’t take an offer seriously without it.
  2. Know your neighborhood priorities — commute distance, school zone, lot size, and walkability all vary by block.
  3. Be ready to move fast — desirable homes in South Orange often receive offers within the first weekend. Having your documents ready is crucial.
  4. Budget for property taxes — New Jersey's property taxes are significant. Factor the full PITI payment into your budget.
  5. Don’t skip the inspection — many South Orange homes are 50–80 years old; a thorough inspection protects you.
