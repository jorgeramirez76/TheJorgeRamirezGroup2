Market Report · March 2026
South Orange, NJ is a charming township that offers a unique blend of urban and suburban living. Located just 15 miles west of New York City, South Orange is a popular destination for homebuyers who want to balance the convenience of city living with the comfort of a small-town atmosphere. As a seasoned real estate expert, I, Jorge Ramirez, have had the pleasure of guiding numerous clients through the process of finding their dream home in this beautiful township. In this neighborhood spotlight guide, we'll explore 3-5 distinct neighborhoods/areas in South Orange, highlighting their vibe, price range, and suitability for different types of buyers. **1. Downtown South Orange** The downtown area of South Orange is the heartbeat of the township, offering a vibrant and bustling atmosphere that's perfect for young professionals and singles. This neighborhood is surrounded by an array of shops, restaurants, and entertainment venues, making it an ideal spot for those who crave the energy of urban living. Home prices in downtown South Orange range from $300,000 to $600,000, with a mix of historic homes, condos, and newly constructed apartments. The best part? You can easily walk to the train station, making your daily commute to NYC a breeze. Families might find the downtown area a bit too lively, but it's an excellent choice for singles, couples, or downsizers who want to be in the midst of the action. **2. Maplewood Village** Just a stone's throw from downtown South Orange lies the picturesque Maplewood Village, a quaint neighborhood that embodies the essence of small-town America. Tree-lined streets, beautiful parks, and a strong sense of community make this area a haven for families. Home prices in Maplewood Village range from $400,000 to $800,000, with a variety of colonial-style homes, many of which have been beautifully updated to meet the needs of modern families. This neighborhood is best suited for families, with its excellent schools, playgrounds, and the annual Maplewood Village Fair, which attracts visitors from all over the region. If you're looking for a warm, welcoming community where your kids can grow and thrive, Maplewood Village should be at the top of your list. **3. Gaslight Commons** Gaslight Commons is an upscale neighborhood in South Orange, known for its stunning gaslight-style street lamps and beautiful, historic homes. This area exudes an elegant, sophisticated vibe, making it a magnet for professionals and downsizers who appreciate refinement and luxury. Home prices in Gaslight Commons range from $600,000 to $1,000,000, with many homes featuring exquisite architectural details, expansive yards, and beautifully manicured gardens. While it's an excellent choice for couples or downsizers who value privacy and prestige, families might find the area a bit too formal. However, the excellent schools and safe, quiet streets make it a viable option for families with older children. **4. Valley Street** Valley Street is a charming, up-and-coming neighborhood in South Orange that's rapidly gaining popularity among homebuyers. This area offers a unique blend of affordability and charm, with a mix of older and newer homes, many of which have been beautifully renovated. Home prices on Valley Street range from $250,000 to $500,000, making it an attractive option for first-time buyers, singles, or couples. The neighborhood is dotted with small parks, playgrounds, and a strong sense of community, making it an excellent choice for families with young children. While it's not as polished as some of the other neighborhoods in South Orange, Valley Street's laid-back, small-town vibe and affordable prices make it a hidden gem that's definitely worth exploring. In conclusion, South Orange, NJ offers a diverse range of neighborhoods that cater to different lifestyles, budgets, and preferences. Whether you're a young professional, a growing family, or a downsizer, there's a spot in South Orange that's perfect for you.
