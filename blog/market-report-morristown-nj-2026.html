Morristown, NJ Real Estate Market Report — March 2026

By Jorge Ramirez | March 2026

Have you been wondering how the Morristown, NJ real estate market is shaping up for 2026? Whether you're contemplating buying, selling, or simply keeping an eye on your home's value, this report is designed to provide you with the insights you need to make informed decisions.

Market Snapshot — Morristown NJ (March 2026)

Out of curiosity, have you noticed any changes in the local market lately? Here's a quick rundown:

  • Median Home Price: $540K
  • Year-Over-Year Change: +3.2%
  • Average Days on Market: 28–38 days
  • Market Type: Balanced market
  • County: Morris County

What's Driving the Morristown Market?

Have you ever marveled at how Morristown manages to blend American history with a vibrant, modern downtown? The town’s commitment to restaurants, nightlife, and a thriving business community has made it a beloved destination. The commute advantage, with the NJ Transit Morris & Essex Line offering a ~50-minute journey to NYC, continues to be a significant draw for professionals, especially as hybrid work schedules emphasize the importance of high-quality commutes.

Help me understand, do you know about the Morris School District? It remains a powerful driver of demand, with families consistently prioritizing it when relocating to the area. This creates a stable floor of buyer demand, regardless of broader market conditions.

Inventory & Competition

What's been the biggest challenge with finding a home in Morristown? Inventory remains somewhat elevated compared to peak years, giving buyers more choices. The neighborhoods most in demand include the Historic District, the Madison Ave corridor, and the Speedwell Ave area.

Buyers have more room to negotiate than in recent years, though well-priced homes still move quickly. It’s important to remember not every home is negotiable, so being prepared can give you an edge.

Price Trends

How open would you be to knowing that Morristown continues to build equity for homeowners with +3.2% year-over-year appreciation? This growth reflects the town's desirability, limited housing stock, and strong commuter appeal. Looking ahead to the second half of 2026, I expect prices to remain firm, with modest continued appreciation in the 3–5% range.

Advice for Buyers

Have you considered getting pre-approved before touring homes? A conditional offer is less competitive in this market. Here are a few more tips:

  • Focus on neighborhoods near the Historic District for best resale value
  • Budget carefully for NJ property taxes — they're significant in Morristown
  • Don't skip the home inspection even under offer pressure

Advice for Sellers

Have you thought about how to price your home effectively? Here are some recommendations:

  • Price based on recent comparable sales — not wishful thinking or Zillow estimates
  • Invest in professional photography; 95% of buyers start their search online
  • Spring (March–June) remains the peak window for maximum buyer exposure
  • Consider pre-listing inspections to remove buyer objections upfront

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Morristown NJ a buyer's or seller's market in 2026?

Morristown is currently a balanced market. Buyers have somewhat more negotiating power, but well-priced homes still move quickly.

What is the median home price in Morristown NJ in 2026?

The median home price in Morristown as of early 2026 is approximately $540K, representing +3.2% year-over-year appreciation. Prices vary by neighborhood, size, and condition.

How fast are homes selling in Morristown NJ?

Homes in Morristown are averaging 28–38 days on market before going under contract. Properly priced, well-presented homes often sell within the first two weeks of listing.

Is now a good time to buy or sell in Morristown NJ?

In a balanced market with +3.2% appreciation, both buyers and sellers have factors to weigh. Sellers benefit from strong demand and rising prices; buyers benefit from locking in before further appreciation. Your specific timeline and goals matter most — a local expert can help you decide.
