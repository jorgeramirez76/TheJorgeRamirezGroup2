Buying a Home in Kenilworth NJ 2026 — Complete Buyer's Guide

As a seasoned real estate agent in New Jersey, I've seen many buyers and sellers navigate the market in Kenilworth. In 2026, this charming town in Union County offers an attractive blend of affordability, community pride, and convenience. If you're considering buying a home in Kenilworth, this guide is for you. I'll cover the town's appeal, current market conditions, neighborhoods, schools, commute, and provide a step-by-step guide on how to buy a home here.

Why Kenilworth NJ Is Worth Considering in 2026

Kenilworth's appeal lies in its balance of small-town charm and big-city access. Located near major highways and with a commute time of 40-50 minutes to New York City via NJ Transit bus to Newark Penn and then train to NYC, or by driving to nearby Cranford or Westfield NJ Transit stations, it's an ideal spot for those who need to reach the city regularly. The town also boasts a strong sense of community, with local events and a good quality of life, making it an excellent choice for families and professionals alike.

Current Market Conditions

The current market in Kenilworth is characterized by a median home price of $445,000, with homes spending an average of 22-30 days on the market. This indicates a seller's market, where homes are in demand and sell relatively quickly. The inventory level is low, which can sometimes lead to a competitive environment for buyers. However, with a year-over-year price change of +6%, Kenilworth is seeing steady growth, making it an attractive investment opportunity.

Here are some key statistics to consider:

  • Median Home Price: $445,000
  • Average Days on Market: 22-30 days
  • Inventory Level: Low
  • Year-over-Year Price Change: +6%

Neighborhoods in Kenilworth

Kenilworth is divided into several neighborhoods, each with its unique character. The Boulevard area is the main residential corridor, known for its beautiful homes and tree-lined streets. South Kenilworth, near the Union border, offers a mix of older and newer homes, with a strong sense of community. North Kenilworth, near the Cranford border, is another popular area, with easy access to amenities and transportation. Lastly, the Kenilworth industrial district, located in the southwest, is an area of commercial and industrial development, reflecting the town's economic diversity.

Union County Schools — Kenilworth School District

The Kenilworth Public Schools serve the town, providing a comprehensive education from elementary through high school. Notably, Governor Livingston High School, which is shared with Berkeley Heights, offers a strong curriculum and extracurricular activities, preparing students well for college and beyond. The district's commitment to education is evident in its facilities and programs, making Kenilworth an attractive option for families with school-age children.

Commute from Kenilworth to NYC

The commute from Kenilworth to New York City is relatively straightforward. By taking the NJ Transit bus to Newark Penn and then the train to NYC, or by driving to the nearby Cranford or Westfield NJ Transit stations and taking the train, you can reach the city in about 40-50 minutes. This commute time, combined with the town's affordable housing options, makes Kenilworth a practical choice for those who work in the city but prefer a more suburban lifestyle.

  2. Get Pre-Approved: Contact a lender to get pre-approved for a mortgage, which will give you an idea of your budget.
  3. Find a Real Estate Agent: Work with a local agent, like myself, who knows the Kenilworth market and can guide you through the process.
  4. View Homes: Visit potential homes, considering factors like location, condition, and price.
  5. Make an Offer: Once you find the right home, your agent will help you make a competitive offer.
  6. Closing: After your offer is accepted, you'll enter the closing process, which includes inspections, appraisals, and finalizing your mortgage.
