Springfield NJ Market Snapshot — March 2026

Have you been keeping an eye on the real estate market in Springfield, NJ lately? Out of curiosity, what's been the biggest challenge you've noticed in finding the right home or deciding to sell?

The Springfield, NJ real estate market as of March 2026 is quite active, with a median home price of $560,000 and an average of 20-28 days on market. The inventory is low, making it a seller's market with a year-over-year price change of +6%. This means competition is fierce, but opportunities abound for those who know what to look for.

Let's break down some of the current stats:

  • Median Home Price: $560,000
  • Average Days on Market: 20-28 days
  • Inventory Level: low
  • Year-over-Year Price Change: +6%

Year-over-Year Price Trends

The +6% year-over-year price change indicates a steady increase, driven by low inventory and high demand. This trend is expected to continue, with buyers competing for the limited available properties. Sellers can anticipate multiple offers, while buyers need to be ready to act quickly.

Neighborhood Breakdown

Springfield is divided into several neighborhoods, each with unique characteristics. The Mountain Ave / Watchung Reservation border area, known for its proximity to the Watchung Reservation, offers prices ranging from $500,000 to $700,000. The Shunpike Road corridor is another popular area, with prices from $450,000 to $650,000.

The Valley Road area is more affordable, with prices ranging from $400,000 to $600,000. The South Springfield near Millburn border area is also in high demand, with prices from $500,000 to $700,000.

Buyer vs Seller Market Analysis

Given the current market conditions, sellers have the upper hand. With low inventory and high demand, sellers can expect multiple offers and competitive bidding. For buyers, this means being prepared to negotiate and potentially offering above asking price.

What's Driving Demand in Springfield

The commute to NYC is a significant factor in driving demand. While there is no direct train, the ~10-minute drive to Summit or Millburn NJ Transit stations provides easy access to Midtown Direct trains, which take about 35-40 minutes to NYC. The strong school district, Jonathan Dayton High School, and proximity to the Watchung Reservation also contribute to the area's appeal.

The lifestyle in Springfield is another draw, with plenty of outdoor activities, restaurants, and shops. The low crime rate and convenient highway access via I-78 and Route 22 make it an attractive choice for buyers.

2026 Forecast

For the rest of 2026, the market is expected to continue its current trend. Low inventory and high demand will likely result in top dollar for sellers and quick action for buyers. The commute to NYC and the strong school district will remain key drivers, while the desirable lifestyle in Springfield will continue to attract potential homeowners.
