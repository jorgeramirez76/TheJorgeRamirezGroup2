Springfield NJ Market Snapshot — March 2026

As of March 2026, the Springfield, NJ real estate market is characterized by a median home price of $560,000, with an average of 20-28 days on market. The inventory level is low, and the market type is a seller's market, with a year-over-year price change of +6%.

Here are the current stats:

  • Median Home Price: $560,000
  • Average Days on Market: 20-28 days
  • Inventory Level: low
  • Year-over-Year Price Change: +6%

Year-over-Year Price Trends

The year-over-year price change of +6% indicates a steady increase in home prices, which is a positive trend for sellers. This increase is driven by the low inventory and high demand in the area.

This trend is expected to continue, with buyers competing for the limited available properties. As a result, sellers can expect to receive multiple offers on their properties, and buyers should be prepared to act quickly when finding a property that meets their needs.

Neighborhood Breakdown

The Springfield, NJ area can be divided into several neighborhoods, each with its unique characteristics and price ranges. The Mountain Ave / Watchung Reservation border area is known for its proximity to the Watchung Reservation, with prices ranging from $500,000 to $700,000. The Shunpike Road corridor is another popular area, with prices ranging from $450,000 to $650,000.

The Valley Road area is a bit more affordable, with prices ranging from $400,000 to $600,000. The South Springfield near Millburn border area is also in high demand, with prices ranging from $500,000 to $700,000.

Buyer vs Seller Market Analysis

As mentioned earlier, the current market is a seller's market, which means that sellers have the upper hand. With low inventory and high demand, sellers can expect to receive multiple offers on their properties, and buyers should be prepared to act quickly when finding a property that meets their needs.

For buyers, this means being prepared to negotiate and potentially offering above asking price. For sellers, this means being able to receive top dollar for their properties, but also being mindful of the potential for multiple offers and being prepared to negotiate.

What's Driving Demand in Springfield

The commute to NYC is a major factor in driving demand in Springfield. With no direct train, but a ~10 min drive to Summit or Millburn NJ Transit stations, commuters can easily access Midtown Direct trains (~35-40 min to NYC). The proximity to the Watchung Reservation and the strong school district, Jonathan Dayton High School, also contribute to the area's appeal.

The lifestyle in Springfield is also a major draw, with plenty of outdoor activities, restaurants, and shops. The low crime rate and great highway access via I-78 and Route 22 also make the area an attractive choice for buyers.
