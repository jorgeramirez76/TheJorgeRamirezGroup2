Neighborhoods in Glen Ridge

Have you been exploring different neighborhoods to find the perfect fit? Glen Ridge is comprised of several unique areas, each with its own character. The Upper Glen Ridge area, located around Forest Ave, offers a mix of modern and historic homes, reflecting the community's evolution over time. The Lower Glen Ridge, centered around Ridgewood Ave, is known for its historic district, where you can find beautifully preserved homes from another era. The Clark St corridor, while being more recently developed, still maintains the area's signature charm and community feel. All of these neighborhoods contribute to the rich tapestry that is Glen Ridge, making it a fascinating place to live.

Essex County Schools — Glen Ridge School District

How important is the quality of the local school district in your decision-making process? The Glen Ridge Borough Public Schools are a significant draw for many potential residents, with a reputation for excellence. Glen Ridge High School, in particular, has consistently been ranked among the top in New Jersey, underscoring the district's commitment to providing a superior education. As a parent or someone considering starting a family, the quality of the local school system is a crucial factor, and Glen Ridge certainly delivers in this regard.

Commute from Glen Ridge to NYC

What's been the biggest factor in your commute to New York City? One of the most significant advantages of living in Glen Ridge is its convenient commute to the city. Via the NJ Transit Montclair-Boonton Line, specifically the Midtown Direct train, you can reach Penn Station in just 30-35 minutes. This convenience is unparalleled, making Glen Ridge an ideal choice for those who need to frequently travel to Manhattan for work or leisure. The ease of commute is a major selling point for anyone weighing the pros and cons of living in this charming borough.
