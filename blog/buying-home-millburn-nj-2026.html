Why Millburn NJ Is One of the Most Sought-After Markets in 2026

Have you ever thought about what makes a town truly special? In the case of Millburn, NJ, it's a perfect blend of exceptional schools, beautiful parks, and a convenient commute to New York City. The township's charm, coupled with its highly rated schools and low crime rate, makes it an attractive option for families and professionals alike. As we move into 2026, this trend shows no signs of slowing — the area's popularity only grows as buyers discover its unique blend of suburban tranquility and effortless NYC access.

The desirability of Millburn is further enhanced by community events, walkable shopping, and the picturesque South Mountain Reservation — offering a lifestyle that balances comfort, security, and entertainment. Buyers here aren't just looking for a house; they're looking for a community. Millburn delivers on every front.

Millburn vs Short Hills — Understanding the Difference

Often mentioned in the same breath, Millburn and Short Hills are neighboring communities that share a school district and a reputation for excellence — but each has its own distinct character. Out of curiosity, have you ever pondered what sets them apart?

Millburn proper offers a more vibrant, walkable downtown experience with shops, restaurants, and a strong sense of community activity. Short Hills — technically part of Millburn Township — tends toward a quieter, more expansive suburban feel, with larger homes, bigger lots, and higher price points (often $1.5M–$3M+).

Prospective buyers should consider their lifestyle priorities: the active, walkable energy of Millburn center, or the spacious privacy of Short Hills. Both benefit from the same excellent school district and low crime rates — the choice comes down to what kind of daily life you're building.

Step-by-Step: How to Buy a Home in Millburn NJ

Have you ever felt overwhelmed by the real estate process? The Millburn market rewards buyers who are prepared. Here's the process from start to keys-in-hand:

  • Get Pre-Approved First — Not pre-qualified. Pre-approved. In Millburn, sellers won't take you seriously without it. Know your number before you fall in love with a house.
  • Define Your Priorities — Millburn center vs Short Hills? Walk to train vs more land? New construction vs character home? Get clear before you start touring.
  • Tour Fast, Decide Faster — When the right house comes up, you'll have hours, not days. Your agent should be showing you homes within 24 hours of listing.
  • Make a Strong Offer — In a multiple-offer situation, price isn't always the only lever. Fewer contingencies, flexible closing dates, and a personal letter can tip the scales.
  • Inspection Period — Don't skip it. Millburn has beautiful older homes that can have hidden issues. A thorough inspection protects your investment.
  • Closing — NJ closings typically take 45-60 days after contract. Your attorney handles the legal side; your agent keeps everything moving.

Millburn School District — Why It Drives the Market

Let's be honest: a significant portion of Millburn's real estate premium is the school district. The Millburn Township School District is consistently ranked among New Jersey's top districts — full stop. Have you ever thought about what this means for your family's future?

Millburn High School regularly appears on national lists of best public high schools. The district's AP course offerings, sports programs, arts programs, and college placement results are the reason families move here specifically. Buyers who don't have school-age children still benefit — the school district's reputation supports property values across every price point and market cycle.

If schools are a priority in your home search, Millburn should be at the top of your list.

Best Neighborhoods in Millburn NJ

Have you ever been unsure where to start your search? Here are some of the best neighborhoods in Millburn NJ to consider:

  • Short Hills — The prestige address. Larger properties, more privacy, estate-style homes. Price range: $1.5M–$4M+. For buyers seeking maximum space and cachet.
  • Millburn Center / Downtown Area — Walkable to the train, restaurants, and shops. Charming older homes, tight-knit community feel. Great for commuters who want to walk to the station.
  • Wyoming Section — A beloved pocket of Millburn known for beautiful homes and a strong neighborhood community. Excellent value relative to Short Hills at similar quality.

Commute from Millburn to NYC — The Midtown Direct Advantage

Have you ever considered how your commute could affect your daily life? Millburn's commute to New York City is one of its most compelling selling points. The Midtown Direct NJ Transit line runs directly from Millburn Station to Penn Station (34th St) in approximately 30–35 minutes — no transfers, no PATH train, no subway. For commuters, this is as good as it gets in New Jersey.

Multiple trains run during peak hours, making the commute predictable and reliable. The walkability from the station to Millburn's downtown adds to the convenience — many residents walk to the train in under 10 minutes. For those who prefer to drive, I-78 and Routes 24 and 124 provide highway access to the rest of the state and tri-state area.

Frequently Asked Questions — Buying a Home in Millburn NJ

How long does it take to commute from Millburn to NYC?

The commute from Millburn to NYC via NJ Transit Midtown Direct takes approximately 30–35 minutes to Penn Station. It's one of the most direct and reliable commuter lines in New Jersey.

What is the median home price in Millburn NJ in 2026?

The median home price in Millburn NJ is approximately $1,250,000 as of 2026. Short Hills properties frequently exceed $1.5M, with luxury estates ranging up to $4M+.

Are the schools in Millburn NJ good?

Millburn Township School District is consistently ranked among New Jersey's top school districts. Millburn High School is one of the best public high schools in the state, which significantly drives demand — and prices — in the area.

Is Millburn NJ a good place to live?

Millburn is widely considered one of the best places to live in New Jersey. Top schools, a 30-minute NYC commute, beautiful neighborhoods, strong property values, and a genuine community character make it a perennial top choice for families and professionals.
