The Cranford Market at a Glance (2026)

As of early 2026, the Cranford real estate market is a seller's market with homes spending an average of 20–27 days on the market. The median home price is approximately $510K, reflecting +3.9% year-over-year appreciation. Cranford punches above its weight: a charming walkable downtown, well-regarded schools, and a riverfront park system make it one of Union County's most livable towns.

Neighborhoods to Know

Cranford offers several distinct neighborhoods, including the walkable downtown/train station area, the Lincoln Park section, and the northwest neighborhoods. Whether you're looking for walkability, larger lots, or proximity to the train, Cranford has options at different price points. I'll help you identify which neighborhood best matches your lifestyle and budget.

Schools

One of the biggest draws to Cranford is the Cranford School District (one of Union County's best). The district consistently earns strong ratings for academic achievement, teacher quality, and extracurricular opportunities. For families, school district boundaries can significantly affect home values — I'll make sure you understand which school your prospective home falls into.

Commute to NYC

For NYC commuters, Cranford's location is a key advantage. The typical commute is via NJ Transit Raritan Valley Line ~45 minutes to NYC. With hybrid and remote work arrangements common, many buyers today can absorb a slightly longer commute in exchange for Cranford's quality of life and home values.

What Buyers Should Know in 2026

Here's what I tell every buyer I work with in Cranford:

  1. Get pre-approved before you look — in a seller's market, sellers won't take an offer seriously without it.
  2. Know your neighborhood priorities — commute distance, school zone, lot size, and walkability all vary by block.
  3. Be ready to move fast — desirable homes in Cranford often receive offers within the first weekend. Have your documents ready.
  4. Budget for property taxes — New Jersey's property taxes are significant. Factor the full PITI payment into your budget.
  5. Don't skip the inspection — many Cranford homes are 50–80 years old; a thorough inspection protects you.
