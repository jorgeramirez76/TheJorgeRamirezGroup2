Why Highland Park NJ Is Worth Considering in 2026

Highland Park offers a unique blend of small-town charm and progressive values, making it an appealing choice for homebuyers. The Raritan River waterfront area, historic district, and Donaldson Park neighborhood provide a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and community events. With its proximity to Rutgers University and the New Brunswick dining scene, Highland Park is an excellent option for those seeking a balanced lifestyle. The town's strong sense of community, beautiful parks, and easy access to NYC via public transportation make it an ideal place to call home.

Neighborhoods in Highland Park

Highland Park features several distinct neighborhoods, each with its own character. The Raritan River waterfront area offers stunning views and access to Donaldson Park, a large riverfront park perfect for outdoor activities. The South 2nd Ave historic district is known for its charming architecture and community events. The Donaldson Park neighborhood provides a mix of older and newer homes, while the Woodbridge Ave corridor is a bustling area with shops and restaurants. These neighborhoods showcase the diversity and charm of Highland Park.

Middlesex County Schools — Highland Park School District

The Highland Park School District serves the community, providing a strong education system. With a focus on academic excellence and community involvement, the district is committed to preparing students for success. Notable schools in the district include Highland Park High School, which offers a comprehensive curriculum and extracurricular activities.

Commute from Highland Park to NYC

Highland Park is conveniently located near several transportation options, making the commute to NYC easy. The NJ Transit bus line provides a 50-60 minute journey to New York City, while the nearby New Brunswick NEC rail station offers a ~20 minute trip. This accessibility is a significant advantage for those working in the city or looking for a quick getaway.
