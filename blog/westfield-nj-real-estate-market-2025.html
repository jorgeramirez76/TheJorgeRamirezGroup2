Westfield NJ Real Estate Market 2025 — Seller's Guide & Report

By Jorge Ramirez | March 23, 2025 | ← Back to Blog

Westfield, NJ is one of Union County's most sought-after communities. The walkable downtown, top-ranked schools, and direct NJ Transit access to NYC make it consistently one of the most competitive markets in the state. If you're thinking about selling in 2025, here's the full picture.

Westfield NJ Housing Market Overview: 2025

Key Market Stats

  • Median sale price: Approximately $850,000–$1,100,000 (varies significantly by style, size, and neighborhood)
  • Average days on market: 14–30 days for well-priced homes; longer for overpriced inventory
  • Sale-to-list price ratio: Many homes selling at or above asking in good condition
  • Inventory: Persistently low — typically under 2 months of supply
  • Year-over-year appreciation: Estimated 3–6% in most price ranges

Who Is Buying in Westfield Right Now?

  • NYC commuters — Westfield's direct train to Manhattan (Penn Station ~1 hour) drives strong demand from NYC professionals priced out of Brooklyn and Manhattan
  • Move-up buyers from Cranford, Clark, and Scotch Plains trading up for the school district
  • Families with school-age children — Westfield public schools are consistently rated top 5% in NJ
  • Downsizers who want to stay in town but right-size their home

Westfield Home Values by Property Type (2025)

Colonial and Center Hall Colonials

The most popular home style in Westfield. 4-bedroom colonials with updated kitchens and baths in the $850K–$1.2M range move quickly. The right prep and pricing can generate multiple offers.

Tudors and Cape Cods

Classic Westfield architecture. Tudors with good bones and updated interiors are getting strong buyer interest. Capes can be trickier — bedroom count and second-floor finish matter a lot here.

Ranches

Smaller buyer pool but dedicated demand, particularly from downsizers and those seeking one-story living. Well-maintained ranches in Westfield are selling well, particularly with updated kitchens and open floor plans.

New Construction and Gut Renovations

High demand and premium prices. Buyers in Westfield are willing to pay $1.3M–$1.8M+ for fully renovated or new-build homes. If your home needs significant work, be realistic about price — buyers with this budget expect turnkey.

Westfield's Neighborhoods: What Buyers Are Looking For

Downtown / In-Town

Walking distance to Westfield's vibrant downtown (restaurants, shops, theater, train station) commands a premium. If you're in-town, that's a major selling point to lead with in your marketing.

North Side / McKinley School District Area

Highly desirable, larger lots, consistent demand.

South Side / Washington School and Tamaques Areas

Strong family demand, mix of price points, good inventory absorption.

What's Selling Fast in Westfield Right Now

  • Updated colonials priced at or slightly below the "feels fair" market number
  • Homes with updated kitchens — white/off-white shaker cabinets, quartz counters, stainless appliances
  • Primary suites with updated baths
  • Homes with home office space (dedicated room or finished basement)
  • Properties with garages and usable outdoor living space

What's Sitting on the Market

  • Overpriced homes that are stretching above recent comparable sales
  • Homes with major deferred maintenance without a price adjustment
  • Dated kitchens and baths in the $900K+ price range — buyers at this level expect more
  • Poor listing photos — this is a well-educated, high-income buyer pool that will pass on a home that doesn't present well online

Preparing Your Westfield Home for Sale in 2025

Strategic Prep Investments That Pay Off

  • Kitchen refresh: New hardware, paint cabinets, fresh countertops if needed — $5K–$15K can add $30K–$50K in perceived value
  • Primary bath update: New fixtures, fresh grout, updated vanity lighting
  • Fresh neutral paint throughout
  • Curb appeal: Westfield buyers often walk the neighborhood before scheduling showings — make a great first impression
  • Pre-listing inspection: Know what's coming before buyers use inspection findings as leverage
