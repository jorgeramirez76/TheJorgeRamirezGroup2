Warren Township NJ Market Snapshot — March 2026

Last updated  ·  April 2026

Out of curiosity, have you been keeping an eye on the real estate market in Warren Township lately? The numbers seem to be telling an interesting story. It's a seller's market, and I bet you've noticed the prices creeping up. Let's dive into what that means for the current state of the market.

Here's a quick stats block to summarize the current state of the market:

  • Median Home Price: $780,000
  • Average Days on Market: 28-38 days
  • Inventory Level: low
  • Year-over-Year Price Change: +5%

These numbers set the stage for a comprehensive analysis of the Warren Township real estate market. Let's break down the key components that are driving this market.

Year-over-Year Price Trends

What's been the biggest challenge with the rising home prices in Warren Township? The +5% year-over-year price change suggests a healthy and steady increase in property values. This trend indicates sustained growth, making it an attractive location for both buyers and sellers. For buyers, this means investing in Warren Township real estate can yield long-term financial benefits. For sellers, it signifies that their properties are likely to appreciate in value over time, provided the market conditions remain favorable.

Neighborhood Breakdown

Warren Township is comprised of distinct neighborhoods, each with its unique character and price range. The Mountain Top area is known for its estate homes with breathtaking views of Watchung Mountain, attracting high-end buyers. The Stirling section and Mt. Horeb Road corridor offer a mix of modern and traditional homes, catering to a wide range of buyers. The Valley View area and Washington Valley section are also popular, with their scenic views and tranquil living conditions. Prices in these neighborhoods can range from the mid-$600,000s for more modest homes in the Valley View area to over $1 million for luxury estates in the Mountain Top area.

The demand in each of these neighborhoods is driven by their unique selling points. For instance, the Mountain Top area's luxury homes and stunning views make it a hotspot for executives and those seeking a high quality of life. The Stirling section, with its blend of old and new, appeals to a broad audience, including families and young professionals. The commute to NYC, school district reputation, and overall quality of life are significant factors that contribute to the desirability of these neighborhoods.

Buyer vs Seller Market Analysis

Given that Warren Township is characterized as a seller's market, sellers are currently in a more advantageous position. The low inventory and high demand, fueled by the area's desirability, mean that sellers can often secure higher sale prices for their properties. For buyers, while there may be less negotiating power, the appeal of Warren Township lies in its strong school district, beautiful landscapes, and convenient commute options to NYC, making the potential higher costs worthwhile for many.

What's Driving Demand in Warren Township

Several key factors are driving demand in Warren Township. The commute to NYC via the NJ Transit Raritan Valley Line, with stations in nearby Basking Ridge and Stirling, offers a convenient route for professionals. The excellent school district, with Watchung Hills Regional High School being one of the top-ranked schools in Somerset County, is a major draw for families. Additionally, the low crime rate, beautiful landscapes, and the overall high quality of life contribute to Warren Township's appeal. These factors combined create a highly sought-after location for both buyers and sellers.

2026 Forecast

Looking ahead to the rest of 2026, the Warren Township real estate market is expected to continue its trend as a seller's market, with steady price appreciation. The demand for properties, especially in the most desirable neighborhoods, is anticipated to remain high, driven by the area's lifestyle, commute, and educational advantages. Buyers and sellers alike should be prepared for a competitive market, where properties, especially those priced correctly and in prime condition, will likely sell quickly.
