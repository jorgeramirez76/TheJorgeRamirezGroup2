Neighborhoods in Edison

Discovering the right neighborhood can make all the difference in your new home experience. Edison is made up of several distinct areas, each with its unique character. The North Edison area offers a serene suburban feel, while South Edison boasts a diverse community with a mix of old and new homes. The Metuchen-Edison border area, known as the Clara Barton section, provides a more rural ambiance with larger properties and a strong sense of community. The Oak Tree Road corridor is a bustling cultural hub with various shops, restaurants, and entertainment options. Lastly, the Fords and Woodbridge Ave areas are popular for their convenient location and affordable prices. How would you describe your ideal neighborhood?

Education is a top priority for many homebuyers, and the Edison Township Public Schools are renowned for their excellence. Schools like J.P. Commute from Edison to NYC

One significant advantage of living in Edison is its convenient commute to New York City. Via the NJ Transit North Jersey Coast / Raritan Valley Line, you can reach Penn Station in just 55-65 minutes. How important is a short commute for your daily life and work routine?
