Neighborhoods to Know

Help me understand, are you leaning towards a specific area in Montclair? The town offers several distinct neighborhoods, including Upper Montclair, the Watchung Plaza area, the South End, and Center Montclair. Each neighborhood offers unique characteristics, from walkability to larger lots, and varying proximity to the train. It's important to identify which one aligns best with your lifestyle and budget.

Schools

One of the biggest draws to Montclair is the Montclair School District. The district consistently earns strong ratings for academic achievement, teacher quality, and extracurricular opportunities. School district boundaries can significantly affect home values. How open would you be to learning more about which school a specific property falls into?

Commute to NYC

For those who work in NYC, the commute is a key factor. The typical commute is via the NJ Transit Montclair-Boonton Line, about 45 minutes to NYC. With many adopting hybrid and remote work arrangements, some are willing to trade a slightly longer commute for Montclair's quality of life and home values. What Buyers Should Know in 2026

How would you feel if I shared some tips that I've found essential for buyers in Montclair?

  1. Get pre-approved before you look — in a seller's market, sellers won't take an offer seriously without it.
  2. Know your neighborhood priorities — commute distance, school zone, lot size, and walkability all vary by block.
  3. Be ready to move fast — desirable homes in Montclair often receive offers within the first weekend. Have your documents ready.
  4. Budget for property taxes — New Jersey's property taxes are significant. Factor the full PITI payment into your budget.
  5. Don't skip the inspection — many Montclair homes are 50–80 years old; a thorough inspection protects you.
