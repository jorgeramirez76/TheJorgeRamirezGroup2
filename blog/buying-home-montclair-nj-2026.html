The Montclair Market at a Glance (2026)

As of early 2026, the Montclair real estate market is a seller's market with homes spending an average of 22–30 days on the market. The median home price is approximately $650K, reflecting +4.5% year-over-year appreciation. Montclair is Essex County's cultural hub—art museums, a world-class music scene, excellent restaurants, and a progressive community make it perennially popular.

Neighborhoods to Know

Montclair offers several distinct neighborhoods, including Upper Montclair, the Watchung Plaza area, the South End, and Center Montclair. Whether you're looking for walkability, larger lots, or proximity to the train, Montclair has options at different price points. I'll help you identify which neighborhood best matches your lifestyle and budget.

Schools

One of the biggest draws to Montclair is the Montclair School District. The district consistently earns strong ratings for academic achievement, teacher quality, and extracurricular opportunities. For families, school district boundaries can significantly affect home values — I'll make sure you understand which school your prospective home falls into.

Commute to NYC

For NYC commuters, Montclair's location is a key advantage. The typical commute is via NJ Transit Montclair-Boonton Line ~45 minutes to NYC. With hybrid and remote work arrangements common, many buyers today can absorb a slightly longer commute in exchange for Montclair's quality of life and home values.

What Buyers Should Know in 2026

Here's what I tell every buyer I work with in Montclair:

  1. Get pre-approved before you look — in a seller's market, sellers won't take an offer seriously without it.
  2. Know your neighborhood priorities — commute distance, school zone, lot size, and walkability all vary by block.
  3. Be ready to move fast — desirable homes in Montclair often receive offers within the first weekend. Have your documents ready.
  4. Budget for property taxes — New Jersey's property taxes are significant. Factor the full PITI payment into your budget.
  5. Don't skip the inspection — many Montclair homes are 50–80 years old; a thorough inspection protects you.
