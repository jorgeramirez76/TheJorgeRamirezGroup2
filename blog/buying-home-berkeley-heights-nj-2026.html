Buying a Home in Berkeley Heights, NJ in 2026: The Complete Guide

As a local expert and advisor, I'm often asked about the best places to live in New Jersey, and Berkeley Heights is always at the top of the list. This charming town in Union County offers a unique blend of small-town character and big-city accessibility, making it one of the most sought-after destinations for homebuyers in 2026. In this guide, we'll explore what makes Berkeley Heights special, its commute options, school system, typical home prices, and valuable tips for buyers.

Town Character and Lifestyle

Berkeley Heights is a picturesque town with a strong sense of community. Its tree-lined streets, local shops, and friendly residents create a welcoming atmosphere that's genuinely hard to find elsewhere in northern NJ. The town's character draws families and professionals alike — offering a tranquil escape from city life while staying connected to everything that matters.

What really sets Berkeley Heights apart is its proximity to nature. The Watchung Reservation and several town parks offer endless opportunities for hiking, biking, and outdoor activities. The town has a genuine neighborhood feel where people know each other. Out of curiosity — can you picture yourself living somewhere where the air is fresh, the streets are quiet, and your neighbors actually know your name? What would that kind of environment mean for you and your family?

Commute to NYC via NJ Transit

One of the most significant advantages of living in Berkeley Heights is its convenient commute to New York City. Via NJ Transit's Raritan Valley Line, residents can reach NYC with a transfer at Newark Penn Station — total commute time is typically around 60–75 minutes. For professionals who work in the city but want a more relaxed suburban lifestyle, this is a reasonable trade-off that many families happily make.

Route 22 and Interstate 78 also provide easy highway access for drivers, and the town's central Union County location puts you within reach of multiple transit options.

School Information — Governor Livingston High School

The Berkeley Heights school system is highly regarded throughout Union County. Governor Livingston High School is consistently recognized for its strong academic programs, dedicated faculty, and vibrant extracurricular activities. The district provides students with a well-rounded education and strong preparation for college and careers.

For families with children, school quality is often the deciding factor in a home search. Berkeley Heights delivers on this front — and that reputation is a big reason homes here hold their value over time.

Typical Home Prices in Berkeley Heights

Homes in Berkeley Heights typically range from around $550,000 to $700,000, with some properties above that range depending on size, lot, and condition. The market here tends to move quickly — well-priced homes in good condition typically attract multiple offers, especially in spring and early fall.

You'll find a mix of classic colonials, split-levels, and ranches — many with generous lots and mature landscaping. New construction is limited, which means most buyers are looking at updated or renovated existing homes.

Tips for Buyers in Berkeley Heights

Get pre-approved first. In a competitive market, sellers want to know you're serious. A pre-approval letter before you start touring homes puts you in a much stronger position.

Don't sleep on the first showing. Good homes here don't last long. If you see something you like, move quickly — waiting a week to schedule a second showing is often too late.

Understand the full cost of ownership. NJ property taxes in Berkeley Heights are typical for Union County — factor these in when budgeting your monthly payment. Your agent should help you run the full numbers before you fall in love with a house.

Think about resale from day one. Even if this is your forever home, proximity to the train station and school district boundaries will affect future value. Buy smart.
