The Morristown Market at a Glance (2026)

As of early 2026, the Morristown real estate market is a balanced market, with homes spending an average of 28–38 days on the market. The median home price is approximately $540K, showing a +3.2% year-over-year appreciation. Morristown offers the charm of American history along with a vibrant downtown filled with restaurants, nightlife, and a thriving business community.

Neighborhoods to Know

Morristown boasts several distinct neighborhoods, such as the Historic District, the Madison Ave corridor, and the Speedwell Ave area. Each neighborhood offers unique features like walkability, larger lots, and proximity to the train. I can help you identify which neighborhood aligns with your lifestyle and budget.

Schools

One of the most attractive aspects of Morristown is the Morris School District, which consistently ranks highly for academic achievement, teacher quality, and extracurricular opportunities. School district boundaries can significantly impact home values, so it's important to understand which school your prospective home falls into. I can guide you through this process.

Commute to NYC

For those commuting to NYC, Morristown's location is a major advantage. The typical commute is via NJ Transit Morris & Essex Line ~50 minutes to NYC. With the rise of hybrid and remote work, many buyers are willing to accept a longer commute for Morristown's quality of life and home values.

What Buyers Should Know in 2026

Here are some key points I share with every buyer in Morristown:

  1. Get pre-approved before you look — in a balanced market, sellers won't take an offer seriously without it.
  2. Know your neighborhood priorities — factors like commute distance, school zone, lot size, and walkability can vary greatly by block.
  3. Be ready to move fast — desirable homes in Morristown often receive offers within the first weekend. Having your documents ready is crucial.
  4. Budget for property taxes — New Jersey's property taxes are substantial, so factor the full PITI payment into your budget.
  5. Don't skip the inspection — many Morristown homes are 50–80 years old, so a thorough inspection is essential to protect you.
