Livingston, NJ Real Estate Market Report — March 2026

By Jorge Ramirez | March 2026

Out of curiosity, have you been keeping an eye on the Livingston, NJ real estate market as we head into spring 2026? Whether you're considering buying, selling, or simply monitoring your home's value, this report is packed with the insights you need to make informed decisions.

Market Snapshot — Livingston NJ (March 2026)

  • Median Home Price: $620K
  • Year-Over-Year Change: +4.8%
  • Average Days on Market: 18–24 days
  • Market Type: Seller's market
  • County: Essex County

What's Driving the Livingston Market?

Livingston has always been a standout for its top-rated schools and strong family-friendly community. The school district's reputation is a significant driver of home values here. With hybrid work schedules becoming more common, the quality of commutes to NYC-area jobs (Drive to NJ Transit or Route 10 corridor, ~50–60 min to NYC) is more important than ever. This makes Livingston a magnet for professionals looking for a balance between city access and suburban comfort.

The Livingston School District (frequently ranked #1 in Essex County) remains a powerful draw. Families consistently prioritize this district when relocating, which creates a steady base of demand even when broader market conditions fluctuate.

Inventory & Competition

Inventory in Livingston is still tight, keeping buyers in a competitive stance for quality listings. The neighborhoods most in demand include the East Side, Center Livingston, and South Livingston.

Homes that are priced correctly and presented well are still attracting multiple offers within the first week. Buyers should be pre-approved and ready to act swiftly.

Price Trends

The +4.8% year-over-year appreciation in Livingston is a testament to the town's desirability, limited housing stock, and strong commuter appeal. Looking ahead to the second half of 2026, I expect prices to remain stable, with modest appreciation in the 3–5% range.

Advice for Buyers

  • Get pre-approved before touring — a conditional offer is less competitive in this market
  • Focus on neighborhoods near the East Side for best resale value
  • Budget carefully for NJ property taxes — they are significant in Livingston
  • Don't skip the home inspection even under offer pressure

Advice for Sellers

  • Price based on recent comparable sales — not wishful thinking or Zillow estimates
  • Invest in professional photography; 95% of buyers start their search online
  • Spring (March–June) remains the peak window for maximum buyer exposure
  • Consider pre-listing inspections to remove buyer objections upfront

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Livingston NJ a buyer's or seller's market in 2026?

Livingston is currently a seller's market. Buyers should be prepared to compete and act quickly on desirable properties.

What is the median home price in Livingston NJ in 2026?

The median home price in Livingston as of early 2026 is approximately $620K, representing +4.8% year-over-year appreciation. Prices vary by neighborhood, size, and condition.

How fast are homes selling in Livingston NJ?

Homes in Livingston are averaging 18–24 days on market before going under contract. Properly priced, well-presented homes often sell within the first two weeks of listing.

Is now a good time to buy or sell in Livingston NJ?

In a seller's market with +4.8% appreciation, both buyers and sellers have factors to weigh. Sellers benefit from strong demand and rising prices; buyers benefit from locking in before further appreciation. Your specific timeline and goals matter most — a local expert can help you decide.
