Summit vs Chatham vs Westfield NJ: Which Premium Town Wins?

The three towns everyone asks about. Here's the head-to-head breakdown you won't find on Zillow.

Short answer: Summit wins on commute and downtown walkability. Chatham wins on schools and inventory scarcity. Westfield wins on space and downtown character. None of them is objectively "best" — it depends what you're optimizing for.

Summit: The Midtown Direct Premium

Summit is the most expensive of the three for one reason: 38 minutes to Penn Station on the Midtown Direct line. That's 17 minutes faster than Westfield and the fastest true Midtown Direct commute you'll find paired with 9-10/10 schools.

What $1.3M-$1.5M buys: A 3-4 bedroom colonial on a quarter-acre lot within walking distance of downtown. Most homes in Summit were built 1920-1960. You're paying for location and schools, not square footage.

The downtown: Summit's downtown is walkable and upscale. Multiple coffee shops, restaurants, boutiques. It's the kind of place where you can live car-free on weekends if you want to.

Schools: Summit Public Schools are consistently ranked 9-10/10. The high school sends graduates to Ivy League and top-tier universities every year.

The catch: Inventory is tight. Summit typically has 15-25 active listings at any given time. Homes sell in 9-12 days on average. You need to move fast and often face multiple-offer situations.

You should choose Summit if: Commute time is your #1 priority and you're willing to pay a premium for it. You want walkability and don't need a huge lot.

Chatham: The Morris County Gem

Chatham is the value play among these three — and I use "value" loosely when the median is $1.0M-$1.1M. But compared to Summit and Westfield, you're getting top-tier schools and Midtown Direct access for $200K-$400K less.

What $1.0M-$1.1M buys: A 4-bedroom colonial on a third to half-acre lot. Chatham homes tend to be slightly newer than Summit (1950-1980 construction) and you get more land per dollar.

The downtown: Chatham's downtown is small and village-style. It's charming but not as built-out as Summit or Westfield. A few restaurants, a coffee shop, the essentials. Most residents drive to Millburn or Madison for variety.

Schools: The School District of the Chathams (Borough and Township combined) is one of NJ's best. Small class sizes, high per-pupil spending, engaged parents.

The geography advantage: Chatham's original Colonial-era lots are non-subdividable. The town's layout physically caps supply, which is why resale holds up even when the broader market softens. You're buying artificial scarcity.

You should choose Chatham if: You want the best price-to-school ratio among premium towns. You're okay with a smaller downtown and you value lot size over walkability.

Westfield: The Space Play

Westfield is the largest of the three towns by population and land area. Where Summit and Chatham feel intimate, Westfield feels like a real town — multiple elementary schools, a downtown with 50+ shops and restaurants, youth sports leagues with hundreds of kids.

What $1.2M buys: A 4-5 bedroom colonial on a half-acre lot, often with a finished basement and two-car garage. Westfield homes are more likely to have been updated in the past 10-15 years compared to Summit.

The downtown: Westfield's downtown is the biggest and most developed of the three. Full grocery store (Kings), multiple banks, professional services, restaurants ranging from pizza to upscale. You can do all your errands without leaving town.

Schools: Westfield Public Schools are 9-10/10 rated. The high school is larger than Summit or Chatham — more AP offerings, more sports teams, more extracurriculars.

The commute trade-off: 55 minutes to Penn Station via the Raritan Valley Line. That's 15-17 minutes longer than Summit or Chatham. Most Westfield commuters are okay with this because they're getting 500-1000 more square feet of house and a bigger yard.

You should choose Westfield if: You want the most space and the biggest downtown. You're okay with a slightly longer commute in exchange for more house per dollar.

The Money Question: Which Is Worth the Premium?

All three towns command a premium over the NJ median ($550K statewide). The question is whether the premium is worth it for your specific situation.

Summit's premium ($1.3M-$1.5M) is worth it if: You commute to midtown 4-5 days a week and that extra 15-20 minutes per day is worth $200K-$400K to you. Over 10 years that's 1,300 hours saved. If your time is worth $200/hour, the math checks out.

Chatham's premium ($1.0M-$1.1M) is worth it if: You want top schools and Midtown Direct access but don't need Summit's walkability. The $200K savings compared to Summit buys you more land and a newer home.

Westfield's premium ($1.2M) is worth it if: You value space over commute time. That extra 15 minutes on the train gets you a bigger yard, more square footage, and a more developed downtown.

What I Tell My Buyers

When families ask me "Summit, Chatham, or Westfield?" I ask three questions:

  1. How often are you commuting? If it's 5 days a week, Summit's 38-minute commute is a quality-of-life upgrade. If it's 2-3 days hybrid, Westfield's 55 minutes is manageable.
  2. Do you want to walk places or drive places? Summit is walkable. Westfield has more stuff but you're driving to it. Chatham is quiet and you'll drive to Madison or Millburn for variety.
  3. How much space do you actually need? If you have 2-3 kids and need room for them to spread out, Westfield gives you the most house and yard. If you're a smaller family or empty-nesters, Summit's walkability beats square footage.

There's no wrong answer among these three. They're all premium towns with great schools, safe streets, and strong resale. The choice comes down to commute, space, and lifestyle fit.
