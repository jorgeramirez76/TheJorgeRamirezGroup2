Market Summary

Morris County's balance of luxury markets (Mendham, Chester) and premium commuter towns (Madison, Chatham) makes it one of NJ's most stable real estate zones. Q2 2026 appreciation is moderate and steady — exactly what long-term buyers want.

As of April 2026, Morris County represents one of the more active segments of the NJ real estate market. Home prices are up +3.5% year-over-year, with strong activity in towns like Madison, Chatham, Morristown, Mendham, and Chester Township — each of which benefits from a mix of NYC commuter appeal, strong public schools, and limited new-construction inventory.

Segment Breakdown

Luxury ($1.5M+)

Mendham Borough, Mendham Township, Chester Township, and Harding are the Morris luxury anchors. $2M+ homes in these towns require longer marketing cycles (60-90 days average) but still command strong prices when presented correctly. Cinematic listing quality matters more than ever at this tier.

Mid-Market ($700K–$1.2M)

Madison and Chatham Borough lead buyer interest. Both benefit from walkable downtowns + Midtown Direct service. Expect bidding wars on move-in-ready homes in May and June.

Entry-Level (under $600K)

Morris County entry-level inventory concentrates in Dover, Wharton, and parts of Rockaway. Investor competition continues but first-time buyers with strong financing packages are winning more often than in 2024.
