Millburn, NJ Real Estate Market Report — March 2026

By Jorge Ramirez | March 2026

Have you been wondering how the Millburn, NJ real estate market is shaping up for the spring? Whether you're considering buying, selling, or just keeping an eye on your home's value, this report will provide you with the insights you need to make informed decisions.

Market Snapshot — Millburn NJ (March 2026)

  • Median Home Price: $950K
  • Year-Over-Year Change: +5.0%
  • Average Days on Market: 15–22 days
  • Market Type: Seller's market
  • County: Essex County

What's Driving the Millburn Market?

Millburn/Short Hills is known for its elite schools, Midtown Direct train service, and a charming village center. The commute advantage, with NJ Transit Midtown Direct taking about 35 minutes to NYC Penn Station, continues to attract NYC-area professionals, especially as hybrid work makes the quality of commutes more important.

The Millburn School District (one of NJ's finest) remains a strong selling point, ensuring a steady demand for homes in the area regardless of broader market conditions.

Inventory & Competition

Inventory in Millburn is still tight, putting buyers in a competitive position for quality listings. Neighborhoods like Millburn downtown, Short Hills, and Wyoming are particularly sought after.

Homes that are priced well and presented attract multiple offers within the first week. Buyers should be pre-approved and ready to act swiftly.

Price Trends

With a +5.0% year-over-year increase in home prices, Millburn is building equity for homeowners. This growth is driven by the town's desirability, limited housing stock, and strong commuter appeal. Looking ahead, I anticipate prices to remain stable, with a modest continued appreciation of 3–5% in the second half of 2026.

Advice for Buyers

  • Get pre-approved before touring — a conditional offer is less competitive in this market.
  • Consider neighborhoods near Millburn downtown for the best resale value.
  • Factor in NJ property taxes, which can be significant in Millburn.
  • Don't skip the home inspection, even under pressure.

Advice for Sellers

  • Price based on recent comparable sales, not wishful thinking or Zillow estimates.
  • Invest in professional photography; 95% of buyers start their search online.
  • Spring (March–June) is still the peak window for maximum buyer exposure.
  • Consider pre-listing inspections to remove buyer objections upfront.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Millburn NJ a buyer's or seller's market in 2026?

Millburn is currently a seller's market. Buyers should be prepared to compete and act quickly on desirable properties.

What is the median home price in Millburn NJ in 2026?

The median home price in Millburn as of early 2026 is approximately $950K, representing +5.0% year-over-year appreciation. Prices vary by neighborhood, size, and condition.

How fast are homes selling in Millburn NJ?

Homes in Millburn are averaging 15–22 days on market before going under contract. Properly priced and well-presented homes often sell within the first two weeks of listing.

Is now a good time to buy or sell in Millburn NJ?

In a seller's market with +5.0% appreciation, both buyers and sellers have factors to weigh. Sellers benefit from strong demand and rising prices; buyers benefit from locking in before further appreciation. Your specific timeline and goals matter most — a local expert can help you decide.
