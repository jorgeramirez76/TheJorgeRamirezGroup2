Summit, NJ Real Estate Market Report — March 2026

By Jorge Ramirez | March 2026

Out of curiosity, have you been keeping an eye on the real estate market in Summit, NJ? Whether you're thinking about buying, selling, or just keeping tabs on your home's value, this report gives you the data you need to make smart decisions.

Market Snapshot — Summit NJ (March 2026)

Help me understand, have you noticed any changes in the local market recently? Here’s a quick snapshot: the median home price is $870K, up 5.2% year-over-year. Homes are averaging 15–20 days on the market, and it’s currently a seller's market in Union County.

What's Driving the Summit Market?

What's been the biggest challenge with finding a home in Summit? The town consistently ranks among New Jersey's most desirable communities, thanks to its outstanding schools, walkable downtown, and one of the fastest train commutes to Midtown Manhattan. The NJ Transit Midtown Direct service takes you to NYC Penn Station in just 38 minutes, making it a major draw for NYC-area professionals, especially with hybrid work schedules.

The Summit School District remains a powerful demand driver. Families consistently prioritize this district when relocating, creating a steady base of buyer demand regardless of broader market conditions.

Inventory & Competition

Have you been noticing fewer homes on the market? Inventory in Summit remains constrained, keeping buyers in competition for quality listings. The neighborhoods most in demand include Summit's downtown village, the Northside, and the Spring Street area.

Homes priced correctly and presented well are still seeing multiple offers in the first week. Buyers should be pre-approved and ready to move.

Price Trends

How open would you be to considering recent price trends? With +5.2% year-over-year appreciation, Summit continues to build equity for homeowners. This growth reflects the town's desirability, limited housing stock, and strong commuter appeal. Looking ahead to the second half of 2026, I expect prices to remain firm, with modest continued appreciation in the 3–5% range.

Advice for Buyers

What's been the biggest piece of advice you’ve heard for buyers? Here are some tips:

  • Get pre-approved before touring — a conditional offer is less competitive in this market
  • Focus on neighborhoods near Summit's downtown village for best resale value
  • Budget carefully for NJ property taxes — they're significant in Summit
  • Don't skip the home inspection even under offer pressure

Advice for Sellers

Have you been considering selling your home? Here are some suggestions:

  • Price based on recent comparable sales — not wishful thinking or Zillow estimates
  • Invest in professional photography; 95% of buyers start their search online
  • Spring (March–June) remains the peak window for maximum buyer exposure
  • Consider pre-listing inspections to remove buyer objections upfront
