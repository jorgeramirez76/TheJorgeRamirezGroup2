Millburn vs Short Hills — Understanding the Difference

Often mentioned en la same breath, Millburn and Short Hills are neighboring communities that share a school district and a reputation for excellence — but each has its own distinct character. Out of curiosity, have you ever pondered what sets them apart?

Millburn proper offers a more vibrant, walkable downtown experience with shops, restaurants, and a strong sense of community activity. Short Hills — technically part of Millburn Township — tends toward a quieter, more expansive suburban feel, with larger homes, bigger lots, and higher price points (often $1.5M–$3M+).

Prospective buyers should consider their lifestyle priorities: the active, walkable energy of Millburn center, or the spacious privacy of Short Hills. Both benefit desde la same excellent school district and low crime rates — the choice comes down to what kind of daily life you're building.

Step-by-Step: How to Comprar una Casa in Millburn NJ

Have you ever felt overwhelmed by the bienes raíces process? The Millburn market rewards buyers who are prepared. Here's the process from start to keys-in-hand:

  • Get Pre-Aprobado First — Not pre-qualified. Pre-approved. In Millburn, sellers won't take you seriously without it. Know your number before you fall in love with a house.

  • Tour Fast, Decide Faster — When the right house comes up, you'll have hours, not days. Your agent should be showing you homes within 24 hours of listing.
  • Make a Strong Offer — In a multiple-offer situation, price isn't always the only lever. Fewer contingencies, flexible closing dates, and a personal letter can tip the scales.

  • Cierre — NJ closings typically take 45-60 days after contract. Your attorney handles the legal side; your agent keeps everything moving.

Let's be honest: a significant portion of Millburn's bienes raíces premium is the school district. The Millburn Township School District is consistently ranked among Nueva Jersey's top districts — full stop. Have you ever thought about what this means for your family's future?

Commute from Millburn to NYC — The Midtown Direct Advantage

Have you ever considered how your commute could affect your daily life? Millburn's commute to New York City is one of its most compelling selling points. The Midtown Direct NJ Transit line runs directly from Millburn Station to Penn Station (34th St) in approximately 30–35 minutes — no transfers, no PATH train, no subway. For commuters, this is as good as it gets in Nueva Jersey.

Multiple trains run during peak hours, making the commute predictable and reliable. The walkability desde la station to Millburn's downtown adds a la convenience — many residents walk a la train in under 10 minutes. For those who prefer to drive, I-78 and Routes 24 and 124 provide highway access a la rest de la state and tri-state area.
