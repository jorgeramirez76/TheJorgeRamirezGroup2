How accurate is a Zillow Zestimate for NJ homes?

Zillow's algorithm relies on public data and cannot account for your home's condition, upgrades, layout, or neighborhood nuances. In Nueva Jersey, Zestimates can be off by 5-15% or more — which on a $600,000 home means a $30,000 to $90,000 discrepancy. A professional CMA from a local agent who knows your market provides a far more reliable number because it incorporates real-time MLS data, pending sales, and on-the-ground knowledge that no algorithm can replicate.

What is a CMA and how is it different from an online estimate?

A Comparative Market Analysis is a detailed report that compares your home to recently sold, pending, and active properties in your area. Unlike automated online estimates, a CMA is prepared by a licensed bienes raíces professional who factors in your home's specific condition, upgrades, lot characteristics, and local market dynamics. It is the same methodology used by appraisers and is the most accurate way to determine what buyers will actually pay for your home.
