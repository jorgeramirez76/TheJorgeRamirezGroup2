What Makes Buying en NJ Different

Nueva Jersey is not like most states when it comes to purchasing a home. Understanding these differences before you start looking will save you time, money, and headaches.

NJ Is an Attorney State

Unlike many states where agents handle everything, Nueva Jersey requires both buyer and seller to have their own bienes raíces attorney. After you sign a contract, there is a three-day attorney review period where either side can negotiate terms, request changes, or walk away. Jorge coordinates with your attorney from day one so nothing falls through the cracks.

Impuesto a la Propiedades Vary Wildly

NJ has some de la highest property taxes en la country, but rates vary dramatically from town to town. A $600,000 home in one town might have $9,000 in annual taxes while the same-priced home two towns over carries $18,000. Jorge will show you the full cost of ownership — not just the purchase price.

Bidding Wars Are Common

In desirable commuter towns, well-priced homes regularly receive multiple offers within the first week. Winning in a competitive market requires more than offering the highest number — it takes strategy, speed, and an agent who knows how to structure an offer that stands out to sellers.

Co-ops vs. Condominios vs. Single-Family

NJ has co-ops (common in Condado de Hudson), condos, townhomes, and single-family homes — each with different financing rules, Asociación de Propietarios structures, and resale considerations. Co-ops in particular have board approval processes and restrictions that can surprise first-time buyers. Jorge will explain the differences before you start looking.

Flood Zones and Insurance

Parts de NJ are in FEMA-designated flood zones, and flood insurance can add thousands to your annual costs. Some towns have higher exposure than others. Jorge identifies flood zone properties early en la process and makes sure you understand the insurance implications before you make an offer.

Preguntas Frecuentes for NJ Buyers

How long does buying a home en NJ take?

Desde la time you go under contract, the typical closing timeline in Nueva Jersey is 45 to 60 days. The attorney review period, home inspection, appraisal, and mortgage underwriting all need to happen sequentially. The search itself varies — some buyers find the right home in a few weeks, others take several months depending on their criteria and how competitive their target towns are. Jorge will give you a realistic timeline based on your specific situation during the initial consultation.

Do I need a mortgage pre-aprobación before looking at homes?

Yes. A pre-aprobación letter from a lender is essential before touring homes en NJ. Sellers and listing agents take pre-aprobado buyers more seriously, and in competitive markets your offer will not even be considered without one. Getting pre-aprobado also helps you understand your true budget — including property taxes, which en NJ can significantly impact your monthly payment. Jorge can connect you with trusted local lenders who specialize en la NJ market.

What are typical NJ costos de cierre for buyers?

Buyers in Nueva Jersey should budget approximately 2 to 5 percent de la purchase price for costos de cierre. This typically includes lender origination fees, title insurance, bienes raíces attorney fees (NJ is an attorney state — you will have your own attorney), homeowner's insurance, prepaid property taxes, and recording fees. Jorge provides a detailed cost breakdown during your initial consultation so you can plan accurately and avoid surprises at the closing table.

How competitive is the NJ housing market right now?

It depends heavily on the town and price range. NYC commuter towns along the Midtown Direct line — Summit, Millburn, Chatham, Maplewood, South Orange — tend to be the most competitive, often seeing multiple offers within days of listing. Other communities offer more balanced conditions where buyers have room to negotiate. Jorge tracks market data across all 138 communities he serves and will give you an honest assessment of what to expect in your target area.

Absolutely. Jorge works with many first-time buyers, particularly those relocating from New York City a la NJ suburbs. He walks every first-time buyer through the entire process step by step — from understanding pre-aprobación to navigating NJ's attorney review period to knowing what to look for during a home inspection.
