# Top 5 Mistakes NJ Home Sellers Make in 2026

## A Guide for Homeowners in NJ

**Published:** February 10, 2023

**Author:** Jorge Ramirez, NJ Real Estate Agent at Keller Williams Premier Properties

**Category:** Real Estate Tips and Advice

**Tags:** nj real estate agent, home selling tips, nj homes for sale

---

As a seasoned New Jersey real estate agent, I've seen my fair share of mistakes made by homeowners when trying to sell their properties. In 2026, the NJ housing market is expected to continue its upward trend, with prices rising and buyers eager to find their dream homes.

However, if you're planning to sell your property in NJ this year, it's essential to avoid these top 5 mistakes that can cost you thousands of dollars and weeks of time. In this article, I'll share expert advice on how to navigate the process successfully.

### Mistake #1: Underpricing Your Home

One of the most common mistakes NJ home sellers make is underpricing their property. This can lead to a long and unsuccessful selling period, as buyers may view your home as overpriced compared to similar properties in the area.

According to data from the NJ Association of Realtors, the median sales price of homes in NJ increased by 10% in 2022 compared to the previous year. If you're priced too low, it can be challenging to attract serious buyers and get your home sold quickly.

To avoid this mistake, research comparable properties in your area using online real estate platforms like Zillow or Redfin. Consider factors such as location, size, and condition when determining a fair market price for your home.

For example, if you're selling a 3-bedroom, 2-bathroom colonial in Montclair, NJ, be sure to compare it to similar homes that have recently sold in the area. This will give you a better idea of what buyers are willing to pay and help you set a competitive price.

### Mistake #2: Not Preparing Your Home for Sale

Before listing your home for sale, make sure it's presentable and ready for potential buyers. This includes:

* Cleaning and decluttering the entire property
* Fixing any cosmetic issues or needed repairs
* Staging your home with furniture and decor to showcase its best features

A well-prepared home is essential in attracting buyers and making a positive first impression. According to a study by the National Association of Realtors, staged homes sell for 10-15% more than unstaged homes.

In NJ, consider investing in some minor renovations or upgrades to make your home stand out from the competition. For example, if you're selling a condo in Hoboken, NJ, consider replacing the carpets with hardwood floors and updating the lighting fixtures to modernize the space.

### Mistake #3: Not Disclosing Known Issues

NJ sellers are required by law to disclose any known issues or defects with their property, including:

* Structural problems
* Environmental concerns
* Pests or rodents
* Lead-based paint

Failing to disclose these issues can lead to costly lawsuits and damage your reputation as a seller.

To avoid this mistake, work closely with your real estate agent (like me!) to identify any potential issues with your property. We'll help you document and disclose these problems upfront, ensuring that buyers are aware of the condition of the property before making an offer.

### Mistake #4: Not Being Flexible on Pricing

When it comes to selling a home in NJ, being flexible on pricing can be key. Buyers often have very specific criteria when looking for their dream home, and being open to negotiations can help you attract more buyers.

Consider offering incentives like credits or concessions to sweeten the deal and make your home more attractive to potential buyers. According to data from Realtor.com, homes that receive offers with contingencies (like a home inspection) tend to sell faster than those without contingencies.

In NJ, consider offering a "clean and open" contract, which allows buyers to conduct inspections and due diligence without penalty if they decide not to purchase the property.

### Mistake #5: Not Planning for Closing Costs

Closing costs can be a significant expense for NJ home sellers, ranging from 2-5% of the sale price. If you're not prepared for these costs, it can impact your ability to sell your property quickly and efficiently.

To avoid this mistake, factor in closing costs when pricing your home or negotiating with buyers. Consider setting aside some funds upfront to cover these expenses.

In NJ, be sure to also budget for additional costs like appraisal fees, title insurance, and attorney fees.

### Conclusion

Selling a home in NJ can be a complex and time-consuming process, but by avoiding these top 5 mistakes, you can increase your chances of success. From underpricing your home to not disclosing known issues, there are many pitfalls that homeowners can fall into when trying to sell their properties.
