# NJ vs NY: Why More New Yorkers Are Choosing New Jersey in 2026

By **Jorge Ramirez**, NJ Real Estate Agent at [Keller Williams Premier Properties](https://thejorgeramirezgroup.com)

In 2026, a growing number of New Yorkers are making the cross-state move to New Jersey. From Manhattanites seeking more space to families looking for affordable living, the Garden State is becoming a top choice. As a real estate agent specializing in NJ relocation, I’ve seen this trend accelerate due to shifting priorities, economic factors, and lifestyle preferences. Let’s break down why New Jersey is outpacing New York as a relocation destination—and what towns like Summit, Hoboken, and Montclair offer that NYC simply can’t.

---

## The Housing Market: Affordability Meets Opportunity

### **Lower Prices, Higher Value**
New York City’s median home price remains stubbornly high, hovering around **$950,000** in 2026. In contrast, New Jersey’s median home price is roughly **30% lower**, with towns like **Summit ($780,000)** and **Westfield ($720,000)** offering comparable amenities at a fraction of the cost. Even in high-demand areas like **Jersey City ($550,000)** and **Hoboken ($620,000)**, buyers get more square footage and better school districts for their money.

### **Diverse Housing Options**
While NYC is dominated by apartments, New Jersey offers a mix of single-family homes, townhouses, and condos. For families, towns like **Chatham** and **Maplewood** provide spacious homes in sought-after school districts. Meanwhile, urban professionals love the walkability of **Jersey City’s Journal Square** or **Hoboken’s Midtown** neighborhoods, which rival Manhattan’s energy without the sky-high prices.

---

## Taxes: A Closer Look at the Numbers

### **Income Taxes**
New York’s top income tax rate (8.82%) remains a burden for high earners, while New Jersey’s top rate (8.97%) is offset by more generous deductions. For families earning $200,000+, the effective tax burden in NJ is often **$2,000–$5,000 less annually** than in NYC.

### **Property Taxes**
New Jersey’s property taxes are frequently misunderstood. While the state has a high effective rate (1.54% statewide), towns like **Westfield (1.8%)** and **Montclair (2.1%)** still offer better value than NYC’s 1.5% rate when combined with higher assessed values. For a $700,000 home, NJ property taxes average **$10,000/year**, compared to NYC’s **$13,000+** for similar properties.

---

## Commute Times: Proximity Without the Hassle

### **Quick Access to NYC**
Thanks to the PATH train, NJ Transit, and the Hudson-Bergen Light Rail, New Jersey offers seamless access to Manhattan. For example:
- **Hoboken**: A 7-minute train ride to Penn Station.
- **Jersey City**: Free ferries to Lower Manhattan in 15 minutes.
- **Summit**: A 35-minute train to Midtown via the Morris & Essex Line.

### **The Remote Work Revolution**
With remote work now standard for 40% of NJ residents (per 2026 U.S. Census data), many New Yorkers are prioritizing quality of life over daily commutes. New Jersey’s suburban charm and low traffic congestion make it ideal for hybrid workers.

---

## Quality of Life: Space, Schools, and Serenity

### **More Space, Less Crowds**
New Jersey’s lower population density means bigger yards, less noise, and more parks. Towns like **Chatham** (population 10,000) and **Maplewood** (population 23,000) offer tree-lined streets and community vibes absent in NYC.

### **Top-Ranked Schools**
NJ’s school districts consistently outperform NYC’s. For example:
- **Chatham School District**: #1 in New Jersey (U.S. News 2026).
- **Montclair and Maplewood-Temple** districts: Known for STEM programs and low student-teacher ratios.

### **Outdoor Living**
From the [Palisades Park](https://www.njstateparks.com) in Bergen County to the [Lackawanna Trail](https://www.njtrails.org) in Hunterdon County, NJ offers year-round outdoor activities. Hoboken and Jersey City residents also enjoy waterfront parks and bike trails that rival NYC’s offerings.

---

## Cost of Living: Saving Without Sacrificing

New Jersey’s cost of living is **12% lower** than NYC’s (2026 data from Numbeo). Groceries, utilities, and healthcare are all more affordable, allowing families to stretch their budgets. For example:
- A gallon of milk costs **$3.20 in Hoboken** vs. **$3.80 in Manhattan**.
- Monthly gym memberships average **$35 in NJ** vs. **$50+ in NYC**.
