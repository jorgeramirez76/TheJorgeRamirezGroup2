What actually drives an NJ home's value?

Five things do most of the work. First, comparable sales — what similar homes within roughly a half mile have sold for in the last few months sets the anchor for yours. Second, condition and updates: a renovated kitchen, a newer roof, or a finished basement moves the number, while deferred maintenance drags it. Third, the school district, which buyers typically pay a premium for whether or not they have kids. Fourth, property taxes — a high tax bill eats into what a buyer can afford monthly, so it presses on price. Fifth, commute: in North and Central Jersey, walkable access to a train line with direct Manhattan service typically adds real value.

Why do online estimates for the same house differ so much?

Because each site runs its own model on public records, and public records don't know your house. Automated estimates can miss a renovated interior, an unpermitted addition, an outdated kitchen, or a lot-line quirk, and they often disagree with each other by tens of thousands of dollars on the same address. They're a reasonable starting point for a ballpark, and they tend to be more reliable in neighborhoods full of similar homes than for unusual properties. Treat any single number as an opinion, not an appraisal.

When should you get a CMA instead?

A comparative market analysis is a human-prepared valuation: an agent selects true comps, walks through how your home differs from each one, and adjusts for condition, layout, lot, and location details no algorithm sees. Get one when you're within a year of possibly selling, when you're weighing a renovation and want to know what it returns, when you're appealing a tax assessment, or when an estate or refinance decision hinges on the number. It typically costs nothing and takes a short visit.

Does timing change what your home is worth?

Somewhat. The spring market typically brings the most buyers and the strongest competition in New Jersey, while winter is quieter. A well-priced, well-presented home sells in any season — but if you have flexibility, knowing your number early lets you choose your moment instead of reacting to it.
