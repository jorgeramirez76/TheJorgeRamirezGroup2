# Contact Acquisition Methods for NJ Homeowners
## Legal and Free Methods Research

**Research Date:** February 9, 2026  
**Focus:** New Jersey Counties - Legal and Free Methods Only

---

## Executive Summary

Acquiring homeowner contact information legally and for free is challenging, especially for emails. This document outlines realistic methods for obtaining names, addresses, and limited phone numbers. **Emails are extremely difficult to obtain legally for free** and generally require paid services or opt-in consent.

---

## 1. Public Records Aggregation

### How It Works
Property records are maintained at the county level in New Jersey. These records include:
- Property owner names (grantor/grantee on deeds)
- Mailing addresses (often different from property address for absentee owners)
- Property assessments and tax information
- Mortgage and lien records
- Sales history

### NJ-Specific Resources

#### County Tax Assessor Records
- **Ocean County:** Free access at Ocean County Library locations
- **Camden County:** Online property records from 1978 to present (free search)
- **Burlington County:** Public Records Search System (PRESS) - free with registration
- **Monmouth County:** Mod IV OPRS system
- **All Counties:** NJPropertyRecords.com (may require subscription for full details)

#### State-Level Resources
- **NJ Property Tax Records Portal:** https://nj.gov/transparency/property/
  - Shows: Location, owner information, block/lot, assessed value, prior year taxes
  - Free public access
- **NJ Parcels:** Limited owner data available (government sources no longer providing full owner info)

#### Open Public Records Act (OPRA)
- Submit formal requests to county clerks for specific records
- **Cost:** Usually free for electronic delivery; small fees for paper copies
- **Process:** Submit written request to appropriate records custodian
- **Timeline:** 7 business days standard response time
- **Resources:**
  - https://www.nj.gov/opra/ - Official OPRA portal
  - https://opramachine.com/ - Tool to submit/browse OPRA requests

### Cost
- **FREE** for most online searches
- Minimal fees for certified copies or bulk paper records

### Legality/Compliance
- ‚úÖ **Fully Legal** - Public records are available to all
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Daniel's Law** (NJ): Limits disclosure of certain public servant home addresses
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Cannot use for purposes of harassment or stalking

### Effectiveness
- **Names:** ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ (Very High) - Always available
- **Mailing Addresses:** ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÜ (High) - Available but may be outdated

## 2. Skip Tracing (Free/Cheap Methods)

### How It Works
Skip tracing locates contact information using various data sources. The "Waterfall Protocol" is a free manual method:

1. **Google Dorks for Public PDFs**
   - Search: `site:*.gov "[Owner Name]" + "Tax" + "[County Name]" filetype:pdf`
   - Finds: Delinquent tax lists, administrative filings

2. **County Tax Lien Sales Lists**
   - Many counties publish lists of properties with unpaid taxes
   - Often include owner names and sometimes additional contact info

3. **Probate Records Search**
   - Check county surrogate court records
   - Useful for inherited properties

4. **Free Online Directories**
   - Cross-reference owner names with public directories

### Free Skip Tracing Tools

| Tool | What You Get | Limitations |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| **TruePeopleSearch** | Phone numbers, addresses, relatives | May be outdated; no bulk search |
| **FastPeopleSearch** | Similar to above | Rate limited |
| **ZabaSearch** | Basic contact info | Limited free data |
| **FamilyTreeNow** | Addresses, possible relatives | Genealogy focus |

### Cost
- **Manual Methods:** FREE
- **Free Online Tools:** FREE (limited)
- **Paid Skip Tracing:** $0.03-$0.15 per match (for comparison)

### Legality/Compliance
- ‚úÖ Generally legal when using public data sources
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Do Not Call Registry:** Cannot call numbers on DNC list for telemarketing
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act):** Cannot use autodialers to cell phones without consent
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **FDCPA:** Fair Debt Collection Practices Act applies if collecting debts

### Effectiveness
- **Phone Numbers:** ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÜ‚òÜ‚òÜ (Low) - Hit or miss; often outdated
- **Addresses:** ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÜ‚òÜ (Medium) - Better for verifying current location
- **Match Rate:** 30-50% for any contact info

### Volume Limits
- Manual methods: Very time-consuming for large lists
- Free online tools: Rate limits, CAPTCHAs, daily limits
- Typical: 10-50 searches per day per tool before restrictions

## 3. Voter Registration Rolls

### How It Works
New Jersey voter registration lists contain:
- Registered voter names
- Home addresses
- Party affiliation (in some states)
- Voting history (dates, not choices)

### NJ Voter Data Access

#### For Political Parties/Candidates
- **FREE access** for political parties
- Can request entire state voter file
- Must be for political purposes only

#### For General Public
- Submit OPRA request to County Superintendent of Elections
- **Cost:** Fee-based for non-political purposes
- **Typical Cost:** $150-$500 per county depending on volume
- **Mercer County Example:** OPRA request required via county website

#### Historical Data
- **Steve Morse Tool:** https://stevemorse.org/njvoters/njvoters.html
- Historical voter records (2007-2023)
- Free search by address

### Cost
- **Political Use:** FREE for qualified parties/candidates
- **Commercial Use:** $150-$500+ per county
- **Historical Research:** FREE via third-party tools

### Legality/Compliance
- ‚úÖ **Legal for Political Purposes**
- ‚ùå **Commercial use restricted** - Most states prohibit commercial use of voter rolls
- ‚ùå **Cannot use for:** Telemarketing, solicitation, commercial advertising
- ‚ö†Ô∏è NJ restricts voter data to political/non-commercial use only

### Effectiveness
- **Names:** ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ (Very High)
- **Addresses:** ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ (Very High)

## 4. Business Registrations (For Investment Properties)

### How It Works
When properties are owned by LLCs, corporations, or trusts:
- Search NJ business entity database
- Find registered agent or business officers
- Cross-reference with other sources

### NJ Business Search Resources

#### Official NJ Database
- **URL:** https://www.njportal.com/dor/businessnamesearch/
- **Features:**
  - Search by business name
  - Search by principal name (officer/director/manager)
  - Search by registered agent
  - Search by entity ID
- **Cost:** FREE to browse basic information
- **Reports:** Detailed status reports incur small fee (~$10-25)

#### What You'll Find
- Business name and entity type
- Business address
- Registered agent name and address
- Officers/Directors/Manager names
- Formation date
- Status (active/inactive)

### Cost
- **Basic Search:** FREE
- **Standing Certificates:** $25-$50
- **Document Copies:** Variable fees

### Legality/Compliance
- ‚úÖ **Fully Legal** - Business records are public
- ‚úÖ Can contact business owners about their properties
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Same DNC/TCPA rules apply to phone contact

### Effectiveness
- **Entity-to-Owner Link:** ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÜ‚òÜ (Medium)

## 5. Social Media Lookup Strategies

### How It Works
Use property owner names to find social media profiles, then gather contact info or reach out directly.

### LinkedIn Strategy
1. Search for owner name + location
2. Filter by industry (real estate, property management, etc.)
3. Check "About" section for contact info
4. Send connection request with personalized message

### Facebook Strategy
1. Search property owner name
2. Filter by location (city/state)
3. Check profile for contact info
4. Join local real estate investor groups

### Instagram/Twitter
- Less effective for direct contact
- Good for identifying lifestyle/property interests

### Cost

## Legal Compliance Summary

### Do Not Call (DNC) Registry
- **Website:** telemarketing.donotcall.gov
- **Requirement:** Must scrub phone numbers against DNC before telemarketing calls
- **Exemptions:** Political calls, surveys, non-profits
- **Penalties:** Up to $43,792 per violation

### TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act)
- Cannot use autodialers for cell phones without consent
- Cannot send unsolicited texts
- Written consent required for robocalls

### CAN-SPAM Act
- Applies to commercial email
- Requires: Accurate header info, physical address, opt-out mechanism
- **Emphasis:** Buying email lists is generally NOT compliant

### New Jersey-Specific Laws
- **Daniel's Law:** Protects public servant addresses
- **NJ Consumer Fraud Act:** Broad protections against deceptive practices

## Recommended Workflow for NJ

1. **Start with Property Records**
   - Use county assessor sites or NJ Property Tax Portal
   - Download owner names and mailing addresses
   - Focus on absentee owners (different mailing address)

2. **Cross-Reference with Business Records**
   - Check if owner is an LLC/corporation
   - Look up registered agent/officers

3. **Use Free Skip Tracing Tools**
   - TruePeopleSearch, FastPeopleSearch
   - Limit: 20-50 per day

4. **Manual Social Media Search**
   - LinkedIn for professional owners
   - Facebook for local connections

5. **Compliance Check**
   - Scrub against DNC before calling
   - Document consent for any email outreach

## Key Takeaways

1. **Emails are the hardest** - Expect to pay or use opt-in methods
2. **Phone numbers are hit-or-miss** - Free sources have 40-60% accuracy
3. **Names and addresses are easy** - Public records provide this freely
4. **Compliance is critical** - DNC and TCPA violations are expensive
5. **Volume requires investment** - Truly free methods don't scale well
