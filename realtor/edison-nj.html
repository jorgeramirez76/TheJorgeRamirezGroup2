Commuting from Edison to NYC

Edison is served by the Northeast Corridor. The primary station is Edison, with a typical Manhattan commute of about 50 minutes to Penn Station or Hoboken (connections vary by train).

What this means for buyers: homes within a 10-minute walk of the station command a meaningful premium over homes that require a drive or shuttle. If the NYC commute is the reason you're moving to Edison, the walkable-to-station home is almost always a better long-term hold — faster to resell, less sensitive to gas-price and ride-service swings.

Ask me for the specific walk-time math before you make an offer. It's the single most underrated pricing factor in Middlesex County commuter towns.
