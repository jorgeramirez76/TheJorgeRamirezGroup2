Union County Real Estate Market Overview

Union County consistently ranks among the most desirable counties in New Jersey for homebuyers, driven by its proximity to New York City, strong public school systems, and mix of suburban character with urban convenience.

The county spans a wide range of price points. On the premium end, Summit homes routinely sell above $1 million — fueled by Midtown Direct train service that puts commuters in Penn Station in roughly 45 minutes. Westfield, with its acclaimed downtown and top-performing schools, sees similar demand. Cranford's charming downtown and Raritan Valley Line access make it one of the fastest-appreciating towns in the county.

For buyers seeking value, towns like Rahway, Roselle Park, and Linden have seen significant growth as NYC commuters get priced out of Summit and Westfield. These communities offer NJ Transit access, improving downtowns, and home prices that are a fraction of their neighboring towns. Scotch Plains and Berkeley Heights attract families with excellent schools and larger lot sizes, while Clark and Mountainside offer quiet residential streets with easy access to the Garden State Parkway and Route 22.

Union County's central location — bordered by Essex County to the north, Middlesex to the south, Morris to the west, and Hudson to the east — gives residents easy access to Newark Liberty International Airport, major highways, and both the Jersey Shore and New York City.

Frequently Asked Questions About Union County Real Estate

What is the average home price in Union County NJ?

Union County home prices vary significantly by town. Premium Midtown Direct communities like Summit and Westfield typically see median prices from $800,000 to over $1.5 million, while towns like Linden, Roselle, and Elizabeth offer more affordable options in the $300,000 to $500,000 range. The county's price diversity is one of its strengths — there is something for first-time buyers, growing families, and luxury seekers. Which Union County towns are on the Midtown Direct train line?

Summit is the premier Midtown Direct town in Union County, offering a direct ride to New York Penn Station in about 45 minutes with no transfer required at Newark or Hoboken. Cranford, Westfield, and Fanwood are on NJ Transit's Raritan Valley Line with convenient NYC access, though they require a transfer at Newark Penn Station. The Midtown Direct designation is a significant driver of home values — Summit's proximity to this line is a major reason homes there command a premium.

How long does it take to sell a house in Union County NJ?

In competitive Union County towns like Summit, Westfield, and Cranford, well-priced homes can sell in under 30 days, often with multiple offers. Less competitive markets or overpriced listings may take 60 to 90 days. The key is pricing correctly on day one and getting maximum exposure to qualified buyers.
