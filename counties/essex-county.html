Buying in Essex County

Essex County attracts buyers who want the best of both worlds: a genuine suburban lifestyle with a fast, direct commute to Manhattan. The question is which town matches your budget, your family's needs, and your commute tolerance.

Short Hills and Millburn represent the pinnacle of Essex County living — nationally ranked schools, stunning homes, and a 35-minute Midtown Direct ride. Expect to pay $1 million or more for a quality single-family home. Montclair offers a more urban-suburban blend with independent restaurants, galleries, a thriving theater scene, and direct NYC trains from multiple stations.

Maplewood and South Orange share the Columbia High School district and a progressive, walkable community feel — with Midtown Direct access and home prices from $500,000 to $1 million. For buyers seeking value, Bloomfield and Nutley offer solid housing stock and improving downtowns at prices well below their neighboring towns.

Livingston, North Caldwell, and Verona attract families looking for excellent schools and larger lots, with a more traditional suburban feel. West Orange spans a wide range — from the luxury homes of the Crestmont section to more affordable neighborhoods near the Orange border.

Essex County Real Estate Market Overview

Essex County is one of New Jersey's most dynamic real estate markets, anchored by the Midtown Direct train line that gives commuters a direct ride to Midtown Manhattan. This single piece of infrastructure drives billions of dollars in home values across the county.

The county's western half — Millburn, Short Hills, Livingston, North Caldwell, and Verona — is defined by large-lot suburban living with nationally ranked school districts. Short Hills routinely appears on lists of America's wealthiest ZIP codes. Livingston draws families with its strong schools and relative value compared to Millburn.

The eastern corridor — Montclair, Glen Ridge, Maplewood, and South Orange — attracts buyers who want walkability, culture, and character. Montclair's downtown rivals any small city for dining and nightlife. Maplewood's village district has become a destination for young families leaving Brooklyn and Manhattan. South Orange offers Seton Hall University, a charming downtown, and Midtown Direct access.

For buyers seeking entry points into Essex County, Bloomfield and Nutley offer solid housing stock at a fraction of Montclair and Glen Ridge prices. Both towns have seen significant appreciation as buyers priced out of more expensive neighbors look for value with good transit access. West Orange's diverse neighborhoods span a wide price range, with luxury sections rivaling Millburn and more affordable areas offering strong starter-home opportunities.

Frequently Asked Questions About Essex County Real Estate

What are the best Midtown Direct towns in Essex County?

Millburn/Short Hills, Maplewood, and South Orange are the premier Midtown Direct towns in Essex County, offering direct NJ Transit service to New York Penn Station in 35 to 50 minutes with no transfer required. Montclair also has direct NYC access via the Montclair-Boonton Line with multiple stations throughout town. These towns command premium home prices precisely because of this commuter convenience — a factor that has only strengthened as hybrid work brings commuters back to the office two to three days per week.

What is the average home price in Essex County NJ?

Essex County home prices range dramatically by town and neighborhood. Short Hills is one of the most expensive ZIP codes in New Jersey, with median home prices above $1.5 million. Millburn, Montclair, and Glen Ridge typically range from $700,000 to $1.2 million. More affordable options exist in Bloomfield and Nutley, where median prices fall between $400,000 and $550,000. West Orange spans a wide range depending on the specific section. Jorge Ramirez provides free, data-driven home valuations for any Essex County property — call 908-230-7844.

Is Montclair NJ a good place to buy a home?

Montclair is one of the most sought-after towns in Essex County, known for its vibrant arts scene, excellent restaurants, ethnically diverse community, and direct NJ Transit service to NYC via multiple stations. The town has distinct neighborhoods with different characters and price points — Upper Montclair commands the highest prices with stately homes and proximity to excellent schools, while areas near the Bloomfield border offer more affordable entry points. Home prices typically range from $500,000 to over $1.5 million depending on neighborhood, size, and condition.

How long does it take to sell a home in Essex County NJ?

In high-demand Essex County towns like Millburn, Short Hills, and Montclair, well-priced homes often sell in under 30 days with multiple offers. The critical factor is pricing accurately from day one — overpriced homes in Essex County lose momentum quickly as buyers in this market are highly informed and compare every listing against recent comparables.
