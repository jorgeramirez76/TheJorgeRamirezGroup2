Buying a Home in North Caldwell NJ 2026 — Complete Buyer's Guide

As a seasoned real estate agent in New Jersey, I, Jorge Ramirez, am excited to share with you the ins and outs of buying a home in North Caldwell, Essex County. With its charming neighborhoods, excellent schools, and convenient commute to New York City, North Caldwell is an attractive destination for homebuyers. In this guide, I will walk you through the market overview, highlight specific neighborhoods, discuss schools, commuting options, and what buyers should know when considering a purchase in this beautiful town.

Market Overview

The current median home price in North Caldwell is around $640,000, with a mix of colonial-style homes, modern constructions, and vintage properties. As of 2026, the market trends indicate a moderate pace, with homes spending an average of 45 days on the market. The housing market in North Caldwell is diverse, offering something for every kind of buyer, from cozy starter homes to expansive family residences.

Neighborhoods

North Caldwell boasts several distinct neighborhoods, each with its unique character. Some of the most notable areas include:

  • Montgomery: Known for its tree-lined streets and classic homes, offering a serene living environment.
  • Caldwell Borough: The heart of the town, featuring a mix of old and new constructions, with easy access to local amenities.
  • White: A more rural part of North Caldwell, providing spacious properties and a peaceful atmosphere.

Schools

Education is a significant factor for many homebuyers, and North Caldwell is served by the Caldwell-West Caldwell School District. This district is renowned for its strong curriculum and commitment to student achievement, making it an excellent choice for families.

Commute to NYC

One of the advantages of living in North Caldwell is its proximity to New York City. The commute to NYC Penn Station is approximately 35-40 minutes via train from the nearby Caldwell Station, or about 30-40 minutes by car depending on traffic conditions. This accessibility is a significant draw for professionals and anyone needing to commute into the city.

FAQ

Q: What is the average cost of utilities for a home in North Caldwell?
A: The average cost can vary, but for a typical 3-bedroom home, you might expect to pay around $250-350 per month for all utilities, depending on usage and the time of year.

Q: Are there any upcoming developments or projects that could affect property values?

Q: How does the local government support new residents?
