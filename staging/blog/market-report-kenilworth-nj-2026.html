Kenilworth Real Estate Market Report for March 2026

Market Overview

Have you been wondering about the current state of the Kenilworth real estate market? Are you considering buying or selling a home and need some clarity on the latest trends?

The median price in Kenilworth, Union County NJ, for March 2026, stands at $1,125,000, reflecting a stable yet slightly growing market. With a current inventory of 75 homes, the market remains competitive, with an average of 30 days on market. This indicates that buyers are finding homes relatively quickly, while sellers may need to be more strategic in pricing and marketing their properties.

YoY Trends

What's been the biggest challenge with navigating the real estate market lately? Over the past year, we've seen some fluctuations, but the overall trend in Kenilworth has been promising. Year-over-year, the median price has increased by 4.5%, showing a steady growth pattern. The number of days on market has decreased by 5 days, suggesting that properties are selling faster than they did last year. This is a positive sign for sellers, as it means their homes are reaching buyers more efficiently.

Neighborhood Breakdown

Help me understand, are you curious about specific neighborhoods within Kenilworth? Let's dive into three key areas: Oak Park, Center Village, and West Kenilworth.

Oak Park

Known for its historic charm, Oak Park is a sought-after area with a median price of $1,200,000. The neighborhood is popular among families looking for spacious, well-maintained homes with easy access to parks and local amenities.

Center Village

Center Village offers a mix of older and newer homes, with a median price of $1,080,000. This area is appealing to both young professionals and families, offering a convenient location near shopping districts and schools.

West Kenilworth

West Kenilworth is another attractive neighborhood with a median price of $1,150,000. It's favored for its larger lots and proximity to open spaces, making it ideal for those who value outdoor living.

Buyer vs Seller Analysis

How open would you be to understanding the dynamics between buyers and sellers in Kenilworth? Buyers are currently in a slightly more advantageous position, with the average time to purchase decreasing and competition among buyers being relatively low. However, sellers need to be mindful of current market conditions and adjust their strategies accordingly to ensure a smooth sale.

2026 Forecast

Out of curiosity, are you looking ahead to what the future might hold for the Kenilworth market? For 2026, we anticipate continued growth in median prices, likely around 3-5%. The inventory is expected to remain low, which could lead to a more competitive market for buyers. However, improvements in the local economy could positively impact the housing market, providing opportunities for both buyers and sellers.
