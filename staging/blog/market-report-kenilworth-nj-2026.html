Kenilworth NJ Market Snapshot — March 2026

As we dive into the Kenilworth, NJ real estate market for March 2026, key statistics paint a picture of a seller's market. The median home price stands at $445,000, with an average of 22-30 days on the market. Inventory levels are characterized as low, indicating a competitive landscape for buyers. The year-over-year price change shows a 6% increase, a significant indicator of the market's health.

Year-over-Year Price Trends

The 6% year-over-year price increase in Kenilworth, NJ, signifies a steady growth in property values. This uptrend is attractive to sellers, as it indicates a strong demand for homes in the area. For buyers, it suggests that purchasing a home in Kenilworth is a sound investment, with expectations of continued appreciation in value over time.

Neighborhood Breakdown

The neighborhoods in Kenilworth, NJ, each have unique characteristics and price ranges. The Boulevard area, being the main residential corridor, tends to have higher priced homes due to its central location and accessibility. South Kenilworth, near the Union border, and North Kenilworth, near the Cranford border, offer more affordable options with a mix of older and newer constructions. The Kenilworth industrial district, located in the southwest, is less residential but still sees some housing, often at lower price points due to the area's zonig and less residential appeal.

Buyer vs Seller Market Analysis

Given the current market type as a seller's market, buyers face a more competitive environment. Sellers, on the other hand, are in a favorable position with the potential to sell their properties quickly and at a good price. The low inventory contributes to this dynamic, pushing buyers to act swiftly when finding a suitable property. Sellers should be prepared for multiple offers on their property and potentially above-asking-price sales.

What's Driving Demand in Kenilworth

The commute to NYC, via NJ Transit bus to Newark Penn and then train to NYC, or driving to nearby Cranford or Westfield NJ Transit stations, is a significant factor driving demand. The proximity to these transportation hubs makes Kenilworth an attractive option for those working in the city but seeking more suburban living. Additionally, the strong local pride, good highway access via I-78 and Route 22, and the presence of notable schools like Governor Livingston High School, contribute to the area's appeal.

2026 Forecast

For the rest of 2026, buyers and sellers in Kenilworth, NJ, can expect the market to continue its trend as a seller's market. With the low inventory levels and steady price growth, sellers will likely maintain the upper hand. However, as more inventory becomes available, the market could start to balance out, offering buyers more negotiating power. The commute times and local amenities will continue to be major draws, making Kenilworth a desirable location for both buyers and renters.
