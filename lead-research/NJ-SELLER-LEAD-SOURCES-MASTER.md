# PART 1: TOP 10 REASONS NJ HOMEOWNERS SELL

## 1. Property Tax Burden
NJ has the highest property taxes in the nation ($9,500-$20,000+/year in target counties). Hudson County PILOT abatements from 2010s are expiring, causing 300-500% tax jumps.

**Indicators:** Tax appeal filings, expiring abatements, delinquent tax lists, tax lien certificates, social media complaints after payment dates (Feb 1, May 1, Aug 1, Nov 1)

**Timeline:** 6-18 months | **Approach:** Lead with tax savings math, show net proceeds vs. burden analysis

## 2. NYC Commute Changes / Remote Work
Target counties are NYC-commuter-dependent. Hybrid/remote work makes expensive transit-adjacent properties (Hudson County) less necessary.

**Indicators:** Employer RTO announcements, LinkedIn job transitions, NJ Transit ridership drops, coworking signups in suburbs

**Timeline:** 3-12 months | **Approach:** ROI, equity capture, cost-per-sqft arbitrage

## 3. Downsizing / Empty Nest
Baby Boomers in Morris, Union, Essex, Middlesex counties with 3,000+ sqft homes they no longer need.

**Indicators:** Home owned 20+ years, Senior Freeze applications, estate planning filings, 4+ bedrooms in family subdivisions, children in out-of-state colleges

**Timeline:** 12-36 months (slowest) | **Approach:** Patience. Honor memories. Quarterly check-ins. Show 55+ community options.

## 4. Divorce / Separation
NJ divorce rate ~2.5/1,000. Marital home is largest shared asset to liquidate.

**Indicators:** Divorce filings (FM cases on NJ eCourts), lis pendens, one spouse removed from utilities, social media status changes, voter registration address changes

**Timeline:** 3-9 months from filing | **Approach:** Neutral, professional, communicate with both parties/attorneys equally

## 5. Financial Distress / Pre-Foreclosure
Job loss, medical debt, ARM resets. NJ has 3+ year foreclosure timeline = long intervention window.

**Indicators:** Lis pendens, sheriff sale lists, IRS/NJ tax liens, code violations, bankruptcy filings (PACER), maxed HELOC

**Timeline:** 1-6 months from acceptance | **Approach:** Empathy first. Educate on short sale vs foreclosure. Partner with HUD counselor.

## 6. Probate / Inherited Property
Heirs inherit properties they don't want or can't maintain. Often out-of-state, want quick liquidation.

**Indicators:** Obituaries, Surrogate Court probate filings, Letters Testamentary, tax bill going to different address than property, no maintenance changes on Google Street View

**Timeline:** 3-12 months from death | **Approach:** Full estate services package — cleanout vendors, estate sale companies, probate attorney referrals

## 7. School District Arbitrage (Aging Out)
Families pay premiums for Millburn, Westfield, Chatham, etc. Once kids graduate, they sell at peak value.

**Indicators:** Ownership 15-18 years in top school district, children's graduation announcements, voter registration age suggesting college-age kids

**Timeline:** 6-18 months after last child leaves | **Approach:** Market to incoming families. Show equity capture math.

## 8. Job Relocation / Transfer
Pharma Corridor (Middlesex), corporate HQs (Morris), NYC feeders (Hudson/Essex). Company changes force moves on tight timelines.

**Indicators:** WARN Act filings (nj.gov/labor), company relocation press releases, LinkedIn job changes, FSBO listings (rushed relocators)

**Timeline:** 1-4 months (fastest) | **Approach:** Speed and certainty. Guaranteed sale timeline. Relo specialist certification.

## 9. Neighborhood Change / Quality of Life
Gentrification (Hudson), density increases, crime shifts, school redistricting, new development impacts.

**Indicators:** Zoning variance applications, crime rate increases, school rating changes, NextDoor/Facebook complaints, Mount Laurel housing obligations

**Timeline:** 3-12 months | **Approach:** Focus on what they're moving TOWARD, never disparage current area (Fair Housing)

## 10. Upsizing / Life Expansion
Growing families, multigenerational needs (Indian, Hispanic, Korean communities in Middlesex/Hudson/Essex), home office needs.

**Indicators:** Marriage records, birth records, building permit denials for additions, home purchased 5-7 years ago, second vehicle at address

**Timeline:** 6-18 months | **Approach:** Simultaneous buy-sell strategy, bridge loans, show equity/buying power math

**NJ Probate Notes:**

- NJ Inheritance Tax still applies to non-lineal heirs (creates sell urgency)
- Class A beneficiaries (spouse, children, grandchildren) are exempt

# PART 4: "NOT SOLD IN LAST YEAR" FILTER

To ensure leads haven't sold recently:
1. **MLS**: Cross-reference against "Sold" status for last 12 months
2. **NJParcels.com**: Check last deed transfer date — if within 12 months, skip
3. **County Clerk deed records**: Search for recent deed recordings on the property
4. **PropStream/BatchLeads**: Both have "last sale date" filter — set to >12 months ago
5. **SR1A Data** (NJ deed transfer records): Available from NJ Division of Taxation, shows all property transfers with dates and prices
