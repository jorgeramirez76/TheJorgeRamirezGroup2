Why People Love Living in Basking Ridge, NJ

🎓 Top-Ranked Schools

Bernards Township School District is consistently ranked among New Jersey's best. Ridge High School is one of the top public high schools in the state, making Basking Ridge a top destination for families.

🌳 Beautiful Community

Known for its historic village center, mature trees, and well-maintained neighborhoods, Basking Ridge combines small-town charm with suburban amenities.

🚆 Easy NYC Commute

NJ Transit rail service connects Basking Ridge to New York Penn Station in about 50-60 minutes. Multiple highway access points (I-78, I-287, Rt. 202) make driving equally convenient.

🏢 Corporate Proximity

Close to major corporate campuses in the Somerset/Morris County corridor, including pharmaceutical, finance, and tech companies. Ideal for professionals who want to minimize their commute.

⛳ Recreation & Parks

Basking Ridge features excellent parks, hiking trails, golf courses, and recreational facilities. The 450-acre Lord Stirling Park and Environmental Education Center is a community gem.

Frequently Asked Questions — Basking Ridge Real Estate

What is the median home price in Basking Ridge NJ?

The median home price in Basking Ridge is approximately $850,000. Prices vary widely — from around $400K for condos and townhomes to $2M+ for luxury single-family estates.

How are the schools in Basking Ridge?

Basking Ridge is served by the Bernards Township School District, consistently ranked among the top districts in New Jersey. Ridge High School regularly earns recognition as one of the state's best public high schools.

How far is Basking Ridge from New York City?

Basking Ridge is approximately 40 miles from Midtown Manhattan. NJ Transit rail service (Bernardsville/Gladstone line) connects the town to NYC Penn Station in roughly 50-60 minutes.

Is Basking Ridge a good place to invest in real estate?

Yes — Basking Ridge has shown consistent appreciation, driven by strong school district demand, limited inventory, and proximity to corporate employment centers. It's one of Somerset County's most sought-after markets.
