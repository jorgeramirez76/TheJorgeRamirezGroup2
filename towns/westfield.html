Home Styles & Architecture in Westfield

Westfield offers beautiful architectural diversity reflecting its development from the late 19th century through the present:

Colonial Homes

The most prevalent style in Westfield, ranging from classic center-hall designs to expanded versions with modern amenities. These homes offer timeless appeal and are found throughout the township, from the historic district to newer sections.

Victorian Homes

Beautiful historic properties, particularly near downtown, many carefully preserved with original details intact. These homes showcase the craftsmanship of a bygone era and are highly sought after by buyers who appreciate historic character.

Tudor Homes

Distinctive architectural gems with steep roofs, half-timbering, and decorative details. Westfield's Tudor homes are particularly concentrated in neighborhoods developed in the 1920s-30s and offer unique character.

Cape Cod Homes

Charming properties, many with dormers and thoughtful additions that expand living space. These homes are popular for their efficient layouts and are found throughout Westfield's neighborhoods.

Craftsman Bungalows

Arts and Crafts-style homes with built-ins, wide porches, and natural materials. These homes offer character and craftsmanship that appeal to buyers seeking something distinctive.

New Construction

Limited tear-down and rebuild activity with modern amenities, open floor plans, and high-end finishes. These homes typically range from $1.3-2.0M and offer contemporary living in established neighborhoods.

Typical Lot Sizes: Westfield lots typically range from 0.15-0.35 acres, with beautifully landscaped streets that create an inviting atmosphere throughout the town.

Westfield Real Estate FAQs

1. What makes Westfield different from Summit?

Both have excellent schools and charming downtowns, but Summit has the direct Midtown Direct train (faster to NYC without a transfer). Westfield offers slightly lower prices and an equally charming downtown experience with exceptional shopping and dining. Both are top choices for discerning buyers.

2. How long is the commute from Westfield to NYC?

The train from Westfield to NYC takes 50-60 minutes with a transfer at Newark. Some residents drive to Summit (10 minutes) to catch the Midtown Direct train (35-40 minutes to NYC). Driving takes 45-75 minutes depending on traffic.

3. Are Westfield schools really as good as people say?

Yes, Westfield Public Schools are consistently ranked among the top in Union County with A+ ratings across all schools. Westfield High School has a 98% graduation rate with exceptional college placement. The schools are a major reason families move to Westfield.

4. What makes Westfield's downtown special?

Westfield's downtown is widely considered one of the best in New Jersey, with upscale shopping, diverse dining options, a historic movie theater, and a true walkable environment. The downtown hosts regular events and creates a vibrant community gathering place.

5. How competitive is the Westfield real estate market?

The market is quite competitive due to limited inventory and high demand. Well-priced homes, especially near downtown or the train, often receive multiple offers within days. Buyers should be pre-approved and prepared to act quickly.

6. What is the typical home price in Westfield?

The median home price in Westfield typically ranges from $900,000-$1,100,000, with entry-level homes starting around $800,000 and luxury properties exceeding $1.5M. Prices reflect the excellent schools, downtown, and overall desirability.

7. What types of homes are available in Westfield?

Westfield offers colonial, Victorian, Tudor, cape cod, craftsman bungalows, and limited new construction. The architectural diversity reflects the town's development history. Most homes are single-family, with very few condominiums or townhomes.

8. Is Westfield walkable?

Yes, Westfield is highly walkable, especially the downtown area where many homes are within walking distance of shops, restaurants, the train, and parks. The town has excellent sidewalks and a pedestrian-friendly design.

9. What is there to do in Westfield?

Westfield offers a vibrant downtown with shopping and dining, regular community events (5K, Arts Festival, concerts), parks and recreation facilities, the historic Rialto Theatre, and proximity to the Watchung Reservation for outdoor activities.

10. How does Westfield compare to Cranford?

Westfield and Cranford share similar downtown charm but Westfield has more upscale shopping and higher home prices (typically 15-20% more). Cranford offers similar schools and a walkable downtown at more accessible prices. Many buyers consider both towns.

11. Are there historic homes in Westfield?

Yes, Westfield has many historic homes, particularly in the downtown area, with properties dating to the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Victorian, Tudor, and colonial homes from this era offer character and craftsmanship highly valued by buyers.

12. What should buyers know about the Westfield market?

Buyers should be pre-approved for financing before starting their search. Understand that the best properties sell quickly. Consider your priorities regarding walkability, lot size, and proximity to schools. Work with an experienced agent who knows the local market.

13. What should sellers know about selling in Westfield?

Sellers benefit from strong demand driven by excellent schools and downtown appeal. Proper preparation, staging, and pricing are essential. Well-presented homes in desirable locations often receive multiple offers. Spring and fall are typically the strongest selling seasons.

14. Is there new construction in Westfield?

New construction is limited due to Westfield's mature nature. Most "new" homes are tear-down rebuilds on existing lots, typically ranging from $1.3-2.0M. These custom homes offer modern amenities in established neighborhoods.

15. What private school options are nearby?

Nearby private options include Holy Trinity Interparochial School (K-8, Westfield), Kent Place School (girls, Summit), Oak Knoll School (girls, Summit), Pingry School (Basking Ridge/Martinsville), and Union Catholic (Scotch Plains).

16. Is Westfield a good place for empty nesters?

Yes, Westfield is excellent for empty nesters who appreciate the walkable downtown, cultural amenities, and the ability to downsize without leaving the community. The town's proximity to NYC and excellent medical facilities also appeal to this demographic.

17. How is the commute to Newark Airport?

Newark Liberty International Airport is about 12 miles away, typically a 20-30 minute drive. This proximity is convenient for frequent travelers and professionals with travel requirements.

18. What recreational facilities does Westfield offer?

Westfield maintains excellent recreational facilities including Tamaques Park, the Memorial Pool, tennis courts, athletic fields, and a comprehensive recreation program for all ages. The nearby Watchung Reservation offers extensive outdoor recreation.

19. Are there any areas to avoid in Westfield?

Westfield is generally a very safe, well-maintained community with no truly problematic areas. Different neighborhoods offer different characteristics – some prioritize walkability while others offer more space. All areas benefit from excellent schools and services.
