Summit NJ Real Estate Market Overview 2026

Summit remains one of Northern New Jersey's most desirable communities for luxury home buyers and NYC commuters. Located just 23 miles west of Manhattan in Union County, Summit combines small-town charm with sophisticated amenities and exceptional schools.

Current Market Statistics

  • Median Home Price: $1.25M - $1.4M
  • Average Home Value: $1,102,034
  • Price per Sq Ft: $518 - $708
  • Average Days on Market: 13 - 30 days
  • Homes Selling Over Asking: 56%
  • Year-Over-Year Price Change: +3.4% to +5.9%
  • Inventory Level: 1.8 months (strong seller's market)

The Summit real estate market is highly competitive. Well-priced homes in desirable neighborhoods often receive multiple offers within days. The luxury segment ($2M+) requires strategic pricing and professional marketing to attract qualified buyers.

Market Trends for 2026

Buyers are prioritizing updated kitchens and bathrooms, open floor plans, outdoor space, and walkability to downtown and the train station. Move-in ready properties command premium prices, while homes needing significant work may sit longer unless priced aggressively.

Home Styles in Summit

Summit offers diverse architectural styles across its neighborhoods:

  • Colonial Homes: The most prevalent style, ranging from classic center-hall colonials to expanded versions with modern additions.
  • Tudor Homes: Distinctive stucco and half-timber designs, particularly popular in established neighborhoods.
  • Victorian Homes: Historic properties near downtown, many beautifully preserved and updated.
  • Contemporary & Modern: Newer construction and gut renovations featuring open floor plans and luxury finishes.
  • Townhomes & Condos: Starting around $420,000-$600,000, appealing to young professionals and downsizers.

NYC Commute

Summit's train station is a major selling point for NYC professionals. The NJ Transit Morris & Essex Line provides direct "Midtown Direct" service to Penn Station.

  • Summit to Penn Station (Express): 39-40 minutes, peak hours, ~$14 one-way
  • Summit to Penn Station (Local): 48-52 minutes, ~$14 one-way
  • Monthly Pass: ~$400 for unlimited rides
  • Driving: 24 miles to Midtown Manhattan (35-50 minutes), ~15 miles to Newark Liberty Airport (20-25 minutes), ~10 miles to downtown Newark (15-20 minutes)

The downtown train station sits at the heart of Summit's commercial district. Commuters can walk from home to the platform, grab coffee at a local café, and be at their Manhattan desk in under an hour.

Property Taxes

New Jersey has among the highest property taxes in the nation, and Summit is no exception. However, Summit enjoys the lowest effective tax rate in Union County at approximately 1.55%. Because home values are high, the actual tax bills remain substantial.

Downtown Summit: Dining, Shopping & Lifestyle

Summit's downtown along Springfield Avenue is arguably the city's most endearing feature-and a genuine competitive advantage in the real estate market. Unlike many suburban towns with strip-mall commercial areas, Summit offers a walkable, vibrant downtown core.

The downtown takes on a decidedly "Old World" feel with tall trees shading the sidewalks, boutique storefronts, and outdoor café seating. Springfield Avenue and Maple Street host upscale boutiques, diverse dining (from the historic Summit Diner to upscale spots like Roots Steakhouse and Huntley Taverne), fitness centers, and professional services.

Parks, Recreation & Outdoor Life

Open space has been essential to Summit's identity since the city was founded. Residents enjoy extensive park facilities and access to the Watchung Reservation.

  • Watchung Reservation: Nearly 2,000 acres of preserved woodland bordering Summit, featuring a 6-mile history trail for hiking and horseback riding, nature programs, and cross-country skiing in winter.
  • Briant Park: 30-acre park with picnic areas, nature trails, and connections to Hidden Valley Park.
  • Wilson Park: Centrally located park with lighted tennis/paddleboard courts, baseball field, basketball court, and playground.
  • Summit Family Aquatic Center: Olympic-sized outdoor pool, swim lessons, water polo, kayak/paddleboard instruction.
  • River Road Golf Course: 9-hole municipal course.
  • Visual Arts Center of New Jersey: Rotating exhibits, art classes, and workshops for all ages.
  • Reeves-Reed Arboretum: 13.5 acres of gardens, woodlands, and nature trails.
  • Summit Playhouse: One of America's oldest continuously operating amateur theaters (since 1891).

Selling Your Summit Home: Strategy Guide

If you're considering selling in Summit, strategic positioning is essential. Based on current market activity, buyers prioritize:

  1. Updated kitchens and bathrooms with modern finishes.
  2. Open floor plans for better flow.
  3. Outdoor space-yards, patios, and landscaping matter.
  4. Move-in condition-turnkey properties attract the most competitive offers.
  5. Walkability to downtown and the train station.

Pricing strategy: Summit's market punishes overpricing. Well-priced homes receive multiple offers within days; overpriced homes sit and eventually sell below true market value. Work with an agent who knows Summit's micro-markets to position your home correctly.

Preparation & presentation: Address deferred maintenance, stage key rooms professionally, invest in professional photography and video, and highlight unique features and upgrades. Effective marketing should reach both local buyers and NYC-based professionals using digital advertising, social media, print exposure, open houses, and networking.

Summit Real Estate FAQs

What is the real estate market like in Summit, NJ?

Summit's market is competitive, with median home prices ranging from $1.25M to $1.4M, average days on market between 13 and 30, and over half of homes selling above asking price.

Are Summit schools good?

Yes. Summit public schools consistently rank among the best in New Jersey, with high proficiency rates in math and reading and an A+ rated high school.

How long is the commute from Summit to NYC?

The NJ Transit Midtown Direct train reaches Penn Station in about 39-52 minutes. Many residents find the door-to-door commute under an hour.

What are property taxes like in Summit, NJ?

Summit's effective tax rate is around 1.55%, the lowest in Union County. However, higher home values mean annual tax bills often exceed $15,000.

Why live in Summit?

Summit offers true walkability, direct NYC access, top-tier schools, a vibrant downtown, extensive parks, and a strong sense of community-all reasons property values remain stable.
