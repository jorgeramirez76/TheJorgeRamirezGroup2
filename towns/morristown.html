Morristown NJ Real Estate Market Overview 2026

Morristown is the vibrant county seat of Morris County, offering a unique blend of urban amenities and suburban charm. Located approximately 30 miles west of Manhattan, Morristown has a rich Revolutionary War history, a thriving downtown, and excellent access to transportation.

Current Market Statistics

  • Median Home Price: $625,000 – $775,000
  • Downtown Condos: $450,000 – $750,000
  • Single-Family Homes: $650,000 – $950,000
  • Average Days on Market: 35 – 55 days
  • Homes Selling Over Asking: 30-40%
  • Inventory Level: 3.0 months (balanced market)

The Morristown real estate market is diverse and steady, offering options from downtown condos to historic single-family homes. The market attracts young professionals, empty nesters, and families seeking urban amenities with reasonable prices.

Home Styles & Housing Stock in Morristown

Morristown offers diverse housing options:

  • Downtown Condos: Modern and converted condos in walkable downtown locations, ranging from studios to 2-bedroom units ($350K-$750K).
  • Historic Homes: Beautiful properties from the 1800s and early 1900s, many with period details and significant character.
  • Colonial Homes: Classic designs from various eras, the predominant single-family style.
  • Cape Cod and Ranch: Popular styles in suburban neighborhoods.
  • New Construction: Limited infill development and townhomes.

Schools & Education

The Morristown School District serves public school students, while the area is known for excellent private options:

Public Schools

  • Normandy Park School: K-2 early childhood center
  • Alexander Hamilton School: Grades 3-5
  • Thomas Jefferson School: Grades 3-5
  • Frelinghuysen Middle School: Grades 6-8
  • Morristown High School: Grades 9-12 with strong AP programs

Private Schools (Morristown's Strength)

  • Delbarton School: Prestigious Catholic boys' school (grades 6-12)
  • Morristown-Beard School: Coed private school (grades 6-12)
  • Villa Walsh Academy: Catholic girls' school (grades 7-12)
  • Academy of Saint Elizabeth: Catholic girls' school
  • Peck School: Private K-8

Higher Education

  • College of Saint Elizabeth: Private women's college
  • Nearby: Drew University (Madison), Fairleigh Dickinson (Florham Park)
