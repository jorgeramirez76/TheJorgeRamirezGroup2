Madison NJ Real Estate Market Overview 2026

Madison is one of Morris County's most charming communities – a true "Mayberry" with tree-lined streets, a vibrant downtown, and a strong sense of community. Home to Drew University, Madison has an academic atmosphere that attracts families, professionals, and empty nesters seeking culture and convenience.

Current Market Statistics

  • Median Home Price: $825,000 – $975,000
  • Average Price per Sq Ft: $380 – $460
  • Average Days on Market: 30 – 50 days
  • Homes Selling Over Asking: 35-45%
  • Year-Over-Year Price Change: +2.0% to +3.5%
  • Inventory Level: 2.5 months (seller's market)

The Madison real estate market is steady and desirable, supported by the town's excellent reputation, train access, and quality of life. Well-priced homes, particularly those near downtown or the train, often receive multiple offers. The market attracts buyers from Hoboken, Jersey City, and Manhattan seeking more space without sacrificing walkability.

Home Styles & Housing Stock in Madison

Madison offers diverse architectural styles, with a charming mix of historic and newer homes:

  • Colonial Homes: The most prevalent style, ranging from classic center-hall colonials built in the 1920s-1950s to expanded versions. These typically feature 3-4 bedrooms and 2,000-3,200 square feet.
  • Victorian & Historic Homes: Beautiful properties from the late 1800s and early 1900s, particularly near downtown and the Drew campus.
  • Cape Cod Homes: Charming properties, many with additions, popular for their character.
  • Split-Level Homes: Found in neighborhoods developed in the 1960s-1970s.
  • Tudor Homes: Distinctive properties with character and charm.
  • New Construction: Limited infill development and tear-downs with modern amenities.

Typical Lot Sizes: Madison lots typically range from 0.2-0.4 acres, with some larger properties near the Drew Forest Preserve.

Neighborhoods in Madison

Downtown / Central Avenue Area

Character: Walkable urban-suburban living with historic charm

Price Range: $750,000 – $1,100,000

Best For: Buyers prioritizing walkability to shops, restaurants, and train

This is Madison's heart, featuring beautiful historic homes within walking distance of the train station, boutique shops, and restaurants. The ability to walk to everything while enjoying a small-town atmosphere makes this area highly desirable.

Drew University Area / Woodland Road

Character: Academic atmosphere with beautiful homes

Price Range: $850,000 – $1,300,000+

Best For: Faculty, professionals, and those seeking beautiful surroundings

Near Drew University and the adjacent forest preserve, this area features impressive homes on generous lots. The neighborhood has a sophisticated, academic feel with tree-lined streets and beautiful architecture.

Ridge Road Area

Character: Established suburban neighborhood

Price Range: $800,000 – $1,000,000

Best For: Families seeking established neighborhoods and good schools

This popular area features well-maintained colonial and split-level homes built primarily in the 1950s-1970s. Many homes have been updated with modern kitchens and finished basements.

Green Village Road Area

Character: Convenient location with diverse housing

Price Range: $750,000 – $950,000

Best For: Buyers seeking value and easy highway access

This area offers a mix of housing styles with convenient access to Route 24 and I-287. The neighborhood is popular with commuters and families.

Loantaka Area

Character: Near parkland with recreational access

Price Range: $800,000 – $1,100,000

Best For: Active families seeking outdoor recreation

Near Loantaka Brook Reservation, this area offers access to hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails. Homes here include colonials, ranches, and split-levels.

Schools: Rankings & Performance

The Madison Public School District is highly regarded and a major draw for families:

Elementary Schools (K-5)

  • Central Avenue School: A rating; serves central Madison with strong academic programs.
  • Kings Road School: A rating; serves northern areas with excellent special education resources.
  • Torey J. Sabatini School: A rating; serves southern Madison with innovative curriculum.

Middle School (6-8)

  • Madison Junior School: A rating; consistently ranks well with strong academics, arts, and athletics.

High School (9-12)

  • Madison High School: A+ rating; ranked among New Jersey's top public high schools with a 97% graduation rate. Notable programs include strong AP offerings, award-winning performing arts, and competitive athletics.

NYC Commute & Transportation

NJ Transit Train Service

The Morris & Essex Line provides direct "Midtown Direct" service from Madison Station:

  • To New York Penn Station: 50-60 minutes
  • To Newark Broad Street: 25-30 minutes
  • Monthly Pass: Approximately $380-$410
  • Peak Trains: Direct service during rush hours

The Madison train station is located downtown, making it walkable from many central homes. Free parking is available at the station.

Driving

  • To Midtown Manhattan: 28-30 miles (45-70 minutes depending on traffic)
  • Major Highways: Route 24 (adjacent), I-287 (5 minutes), I-80 (10 minutes), I-78 (15 minutes)
