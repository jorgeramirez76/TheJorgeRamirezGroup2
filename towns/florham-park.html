Florham Park NJ Real Estate Market Overview 2026

Florham Park is one of Morris County's most unique communities – a affluent residential township that doubles as a major corporate headquarters hub. Home to BASF's North American headquarters, the former ExxonMobil campus (now repurposed), and numerous other corporations, Florham Park offers a rare combination of suburban tranquility and economic opportunity.

Current Market Statistics

  • Median Home Price: $850,000 – $1,050,000
  • Average Price per Sq Ft: $380 – $480
  • Average Days on Market: 30 – 50 days
  • Homes Selling Over Asking: 35-45%
  • Year-Over-Year Price Change: +2.0% to +3.5%
  • Inventory Level: 2.8 months (balanced to seller's market)

The Florham Park real estate market is steady and predictable, with demand consistently supported by corporate relocations. Unlike some neighboring towns, Florham Park sees year-round activity from executives transferring in and out of the area. The market tends to be less frenzied than Chatham or Summit but maintains strong values due to consistent demand.

Market Trends for 2026

Buyers in Florham Park are prioritizing proximity to corporate campuses, newer construction, and move-in ready conditions. The demand for home offices remains strong given the professional demographic. New construction and recently updated homes command premiums.

Home Styles & Housing Stock in Florham Park

Florham Park offers diverse housing options, with significant new construction alongside established neighborhoods:

  • Colonial Homes: The most prevalent style in established neighborhoods, typically built in the 1960s-1980s. These homes feature 3-5 bedrooms and range from 2,200 to 3,500 square feet.
  • New Construction: Florham Park has seen significant new development, particularly in the Brooklake area and along Columbia Turnpike. These homes feature open floor plans, luxury finishes, and smart home technology ($1.2M-$2M+).
  • Executive Homes: Large custom homes on generous lots, often featuring 4,000+ square feet, three-car garages, and premium finishes ($1.3M-$2.5M).
  • Ranch and Split-Level Homes: Found in older sections, popular with empty nesters and those seeking single-level living options.
  • Townhomes and Condos: Limited but available options, particularly near corporate campuses, ranging from $500,000-$750,000.
  • Historic Homes: Some beautiful older properties near the college campuses, including former estate homes.

Typical Lot Sizes: Florham Park lots are generally generous, ranging from 0.4-0.8 acres in most neighborhoods, with some estate properties featuring 1-3+ acres.

Schools: Rankings & Performance

Florham Park is served by the Florham Park School District (K-8) and the Hanover Park Regional High School District (9-12), which includes students from Florham Park and Hanover Township.

Elementary Schools (K-2)

  • Briarwood Elementary School: A rating; serves grades K-2 with strong early childhood programs.

Elementary School (3-5)

  • Brooklake School: A rating; serves grades 3-5 with comprehensive academic and enrichment programs.

Middle School (6-8)

  • Ridgedale Middle School: A rating; consistently ranks well with strong academics, arts, and athletics. The school offers comprehensive foreign language programs and technology integration.

High School (9-12)

  • Hanover Park High School: A- rating; serves Florham Park and Hanover Township students. The school offers comprehensive AP courses, competitive athletics, and strong performing arts programs. Graduates attend top colleges nationwide.

Florham Park residents may also apply to the Academy for Mathematics, Science, and Engineering, a competitive magnet school located at Morris County School of Technology.

NYC Commute & Transportation

Florham Park's location provides multiple commuting options, though it's primarily a driving community.

Commuter Rail Options

While Florham Park doesn't have its own train station, several nearby options serve residents:

  • Madison Station (NJ Transit Morristown Line): 5-10 minute drive; 50-60 minutes to NYC Penn Station
  • Convent Station: 10 minute drive; 45-55 minutes to NYC
  • Chatham Station: 10 minute drive; 45-55 minutes to NYC
  • Morristown Station: 10 minute drive; 55-65 minutes to NYC

Bus Service

  • Coach USA: Service to Port Authority from nearby Madison and Morristown
  • Corporate Shuttles: Many corporations offer shuttle services to train stations

Driving

  • To Midtown Manhattan: 28-30 miles (45-75 minutes depending on traffic)
  • To Downtown Newark: 18 miles (25-35 minutes)
  • To Newark Liberty Airport: 20 miles (25-35 minutes)
  • Major Highways: I-287 (adjacent), Route 24 (2 minutes), I-80 (10 minutes), I-78 (15 minutes), Garden State Parkway (15 minutes)

Florham Park's highway access is exceptional, making it ideal for professionals who drive to work in Newark, Jersey City, or NYC, or who travel frequently.

Florham Park Real Estate FAQs

What is the average home price in Florham Park NJ?

The median home price in Florham Park ranges from $850,000 to $1,050,000 as of 2026. Entry-level homes start around $700,000, while luxury properties and new construction can reach $2M+.

How are the schools in Florham Park?

Florham Park schools are highly rated (A rating). The district serves K-8, and high school students attend Hanover Park High School (A- rating), which is shared with Hanover Township.

Is there a train station in Florham Park?

No, Florham Park doesn't have its own train station. Residents typically drive to nearby Madison, Convent Station, Chatham, or Morristown stations for NJ Transit service to NYC.

What major companies are in Florham Park?

Florham Park is home to BASF's North American headquarters and numerous other corporations. The former ExxonMobil campus has been repurposed for multiple tenants.

What are property taxes in Florham Park?

Florham Park has an effective tax rate of approximately 2.1%. Annual taxes on a $1M home would be approximately $21,000.

Is Florham Park a good place for corporate executives?

Absolutely. Florham Park is designed for corporate life, with many executives living close to their offices. The town offers luxury homes, good schools, and easy access to highways and airports.

What is the commute like from Florham Park?

Are there condos or townhomes in Florham Park?
