Home Styles & Housing Stock in Maplewood

Maplewood is famous for its beautiful architecture, particularly its distinctive Tudor homes. The town's housing stock dates primarily from the early 1900s through the 1960s, with the most distinctive homes built during the 1920s and 1930s when Maplewood was developing as a commuter suburb for Manhattan professionals.

Architectural Styles

  • Tudor Homes: Maplewood's signature style – distinctive half-timbered homes built primarily in the 1920s-1930s. These homes feature steeply pitched gable roofs, elaborate chimneys, decorative half-timbering, and leaded glass windows. The Tudor Revival architecture gives Maplewood its distinctive character and is highly sought after by buyers who appreciate historic charm. Many of these homes have been lovingly updated while preserving original details like arched doorways, wrought iron fixtures, and exposed beams.
  • Colonial Homes: Classic center-hall colonials from various eras, many beautifully maintained or updated. These homes typically feature symmetrical facades, central entrances, and traditional floor plans that appeal to families seeking formal living and dining spaces alongside modern kitchens and family rooms.
  • Cape Cod Homes: Charming properties, often with dormers and additions, popular for their cozy feel and efficient use of space. Many Capes in Maplewood have been expanded over the years to add second-floor master suites and additional bedrooms.
  • Victorian Homes: Beautiful historic properties, particularly near the village area, featuring ornate details, wraparound porches, and gingerbread trim. These homes appeal to buyers who love period character and unique architectural elements.
  • Split-Level and Ranch Homes: Found in neighborhoods developed in the 1950s-1960s, these homes offer more open floor plans and are popular with buyers seeking single-level living or more modern layouts.
  • Contemporary & Modern: Some newer construction and renovations featuring modern design, clean lines, and open-concept living spaces that appeal to buyers seeking a more current aesthetic.

Lot Sizes and Property Features

Typical Lot Sizes: Maplewood lots typically range from 0.15 to 0.3 acres, with tree-lined streets creating a lush, suburban feel. Many properties feature mature landscaping, established gardens, and backyard spaces perfect for entertaining or children's play. The town's commitment to preserving its tree canopy means most homes enjoy shade and privacy from mature oak, maple, and sycamore trees.

Common Property Features: Many Maplewood homes include front porches that encourage neighborly interaction, detached garages (some converted to studios or offices), basements suitable for finishing, and architectural details like built-in cabinetry, fireplaces with original mantels, and hardwood floors. The town's historic preservation efforts help maintain the character that makes Maplewood so appealing.

Schools: Rankings & Performance

The South Orange-Maplewood School District serves both communities and is known for academic excellence, diversity, and strong arts programs. The district consistently ranks among New Jersey's top-performing districts while maintaining a commitment to equity and inclusion.

Elementary Schools (K-5)

  • Clinton Elementary School: A rating with Niche.com score of A-; diverse student body with strong academics. Clinton emphasizes project-based learning and has active parent involvement.
  • Jefferson Elementary School: A rating; known for arts integration and community involvement. Jefferson features a strong visual arts program and emphasizes creative expression alongside academic achievement.
  • Seth Boyden Elementary School: A rating; innovative programs and environmental focus. Seth Boyden emphasizes sustainability and hands-on learning with gardens and outdoor classrooms.
  • Tuscan Elementary School: A rating; strong academic outcomes and dedicated faculty. Tuscan serves the northern part of Maplewood and maintains high test scores and student engagement.

Middle Schools (6-8)

  • Maplewood Middle School: A rating with strong math and science programs; serves the Maplewood side of district with dedicated teachers and active extracurriculars.
  • South Orange Middle School: A rating; serving the South Orange side with similar academic rigor and program offerings.

High School (9-12)

  • Columbia High School: A rating with Niche.com ranking in the top 15% of New Jersey high schools; one of New Jersey's most diverse and acclaimed high schools. Notable for exceptional performing arts (alumni include many famous actors and musicians including Elisabeth Shue, Zach Braff, and others), strong academics with numerous AP courses, championship athletics, and outstanding college placement rates. The school's diversity is often cited as one of its greatest strengths.

Maplewood Real Estate FAQs

1. What is the average home price in Maplewood NJ?

The median home price in Maplewood ranges from $725,000 to $875,000 as of 2026. Entry-level homes start around $600,000, while larger updated homes in prime locations can exceed $1 million.

2. Is Maplewood really as diverse as people say?

Yes, Maplewood is one of the most racially diverse suburbs in America, with genuine integration across neighborhoods and schools. Unlike some communities where diversity exists in pockets, Maplewood's diversity is truly integrated throughout the town.

3. How long is the commute from Maplewood to NYC?

The train from Maplewood to New York Penn Station takes 35-45 minutes on the Midtown Direct line. Driving takes 35-60 minutes depending on traffic.

4. Are Maplewood schools good?

Yes, Columbia High School is well-regarded with strong academics and exceptional performing arts programs. The elementary schools all receive A ratings, and the district is known for both academic excellence and diversity.

5. Why should I choose you as my Maplewood Realtor?

I understand what makes Maplewood special – the diversity, the architecture, the community. I help buyers find homes that fit this unique town and help sellers market to buyers specifically seeking Maplewood's qualities.

6. What are property taxes like in Maplewood?

Property taxes in Maplewood are approximately 2.8% of assessed value. A $800,000 home typically pays around $22,400 annually. While higher than national averages, these taxes fund excellent schools and services.

7. Which elementary school will my children attend?

School assignment depends on your address within Maplewood. Clinton, Jefferson, Seth Boyden, and Tuscan each serve specific zones. I can help you understand school boundaries when house hunting.

8. Is parking available at the Maplewood train station?

Yes, but permits are limited. Monthly permits are available through the township, and daily parking is also offered. Many residents walk or bike to the station. I can explain the permit application process.

9. What utilities serve Maplewood?

Electric is provided by PSE&G, gas by PSE&G, water by the Township of Maplewood, and sewer by the township as well. Trash and recycling are municipal services included in taxes.

10. Are there pet restrictions in Maplewood?

There are no unusual pet restrictions at the town level. Individual condos or co-ops may have pet policies. Maplewood is very dog-friendly with the nearby South Mountain Reservation offering extensive trails.

11. What architectural styles are most common?

Tudor Revival is Maplewood's signature style, built primarily in the 1920s-1930s. You'll also find Colonials, Cape Cods, Victorians, and some mid-century homes. The architectural variety is part of the town's charm.

12. How competitive is the Maplewood market?

The market is competitive with 2.4 months of inventory (a seller's market). Well-priced homes often receive multiple offers, especially those near the train or with updated kitchens. Average days on market is 30-50 days.

13. What's the difference between Maplewood and South Orange?

The towns share a school district but have different characters. Maplewood is often seen as more residential and family-focused, while South Orange has a slightly more urban feel with its own downtown. Both are diverse and progressive.

14. Are there any new construction homes in Maplewood?

New construction is limited due to the town's established nature. Most "new" homes are extensive renovations or rebuilds of existing properties. The town prioritizes historic preservation.

15. What's the best neighborhood for families?

The DeHart/Wyoming area and Memorial Park area are particularly popular with families due to proximity to schools and parks. However, all neighborhoods are family-friendly with active community involvement.

16. How are the restaurants in Maplewood?

The village has a growing restaurant scene including The Woodland, Arturo's for Italian, and various cafes. Many residents also enjoy nearby options in South Orange, Millburn, and Summit.

17. Is Maplewood LGBTQ+ friendly?

Absolutely. Maplewood is recognized as one of the most LGBTQ+ friendly suburbs in America. The town has an active LGBTQ+ community, inclusive schools, and anti-discrimination protections.

18. What should I know about buying a Tudor home?

Tudors are beautiful but require specialized maintenance. I work with inspectors familiar with historic homes and can connect you with contractors experienced in preserving period details while updating systems.

19. Are there any areas to avoid in Maplewood?

Maplewood doesn't really have "bad" neighborhoods. The entire town is safe and well-maintained. Price points vary by location relative to the train and specific architectural appeal.

20. What should I know about flood zones?

Most of Maplewood is not in a flood zone, but some properties near streams or low-lying areas may have flood considerations. I check flood maps for any property you're considering and can explain insurance implications.
