Maplewood NJ Real Estate Market Overview 2026

Maplewood is one of Essex County's most sought-after communities, consistently ranked among the best places to live in New Jersey. Located approximately 20 miles west of Manhattan, Maplewood offers a rare combination of diversity, excellent schools, direct NYC train access, and a genuine sense of community.

Current Market Statistics

  • Median Home Price: $725,000 – $875,000
  • Average Price per Sq Ft: $340 – $420
  • Average Days on Market: 30 – 50 days
  • Homes Selling Over Asking: 40-50%
  • Year-Over-Year Price Change: +2.5% to +4.0%
  • Inventory Level: 2.4 months (seller's market)

The Maplewood real estate market is consistently strong, supported by demand from NYC professionals seeking diverse, welcoming communities with excellent schools. Well-priced homes often receive multiple offers, particularly those near the train station or with updated kitchens.

Home Styles & Housing Stock in Maplewood

Maplewood is famous for its beautiful architecture, particularly its distinctive Tudor homes:

  • Tudor Homes: Maplewood's signature style – distinctive half-timbered homes built primarily in the 1920s-1930s. These homes offer character and charm that's hard to find elsewhere.
  • Colonial Homes: Classic center-hall colonials from various eras, many beautifully maintained or updated.
  • Cape Cod Homes: Charming properties, often with additions, popular for their cozy feel.
  • Victorian Homes: Beautiful historic properties, particularly near the village area.
  • Split-Level and Ranch Homes: Found in neighborhoods developed in the 1950s-1960s.
  • Contemporary & Modern: Some newer construction and renovations featuring modern design.

Typical Lot Sizes: Maplewood lots typically range from 0.15-0.3 acres, with tree-lined streets creating a lush, suburban feel.

Schools: Rankings & Performance

The South Orange-Maplewood School District serves both communities:

Elementary Schools (K-5)

  • Clinton Elementary School: A rating; diverse student body with strong academics.
  • Jefferson Elementary School: A rating; known for arts integration and community involvement.
  • Seth Boyden Elementary School: A rating; innovative programs and environmental focus.
  • Tuscan Elementary School: A rating; strong academic outcomes.

Middle Schools (6-8)

  • Maplewood Middle School: A rating; comprehensive programs serving the Maplewood side of district.
  • South Orange Middle School: A rating; serving the South Orange side.

High School (9-12)

  • Columbia High School: A rating; one of New Jersey's most diverse and acclaimed high schools. Notable for exceptional performing arts (alumni include many famous actors and musicians), strong academics, and championship athletics.
