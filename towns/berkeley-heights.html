Berkeley Heights NJ Real Estate Market Overview 2026

Berkeley Heights consistently ranks as one of Union County's most desirable communities for families seeking excellent schools, spacious properties, and a peaceful suburban lifestyle. Located approximately 28 miles west of Manhattan, this township offers a rare combination of top-tier education, generous lot sizes, and relatively reasonable property taxes compared to neighboring towns.

Current Market Statistics

  • Median Home Price: $875,000 – $925,000
  • Average Price per Sq Ft: $380 – $450
  • Average Days on Market: 17 – 35 days
  • Homes Selling Over Asking: 45-55%
  • Year-Over-Year Price Change: +1.8% to +3.5%
  • Inventory Level: 2.1 months (seller's market)

The Berkeley Heights real estate market remains competitive and active. Homes priced correctly and presented well typically receive multiple offers, particularly in the spring and fall markets. The township's excellent school reputation and spacious properties continue to attract families from Hoboken, Jersey City, and Manhattan looking for more space without sacrificing educational quality.

Market Trends for 2026

Buyers in Berkeley Heights are prioritizing updated kitchens and bathrooms, open floor plans, finished basements, and outdoor living spaces. Move-in ready homes command premium prices, while properties needing significant work may sit longer unless priced competitively. The demand for home offices continues post-pandemic, with many buyers seeking dedicated workspace.

Home Styles & Housing Stock in Berkeley Heights

Berkeley Heights offers diverse architectural styles, with most homes built between the 1950s and 1980s, though new construction and extensive renovations are increasingly common:

  • Colonial Homes: The most prevalent style throughout the township, typically featuring 4 bedrooms, 2.5 baths, and ranging from 2,200 to 3,500 square feet on lots of 0.25 to 0.5 acres.
  • Split-Level Homes: Popular in neighborhoods developed during the 1960s and 1970s, offering flexible living spaces and typically priced between $750K-$900K.
  • Ranch Homes: Found in older sections of town, often on larger lots of 0.75+ acres, popular with buyers seeking single-level living or renovation opportunities.
  • Contemporary & Expanded Colonials: Many original homes have been significantly expanded and updated with modern amenities, open floor plans, and luxury finishes.
  • New Construction: Limited but available, typically in the $1.2M-$1.8M range with premium finishes and smart home technology.

Typical Lot Sizes: One of Berkeley Heights' greatest assets is its generous lot sizes. While newer developments feature 0.25-0.33 acre lots, many established neighborhoods offer 0.5-1+ acre properties, providing privacy and space that neighboring towns often cannot match.

Schools: Rankings & Performance

Middle School (6-8)

NYC Commute & Transportation

Berkeley Heights offers multiple commuting options to Manhattan, making it an attractive choice for NYC professionals seeking suburban living.

NJ Transit Train Service

The Raritan Valley Line provides direct service from Berkeley Heights Station to Newark Penn Station, with connections to New York Penn Station:

  • To Newark Penn Station: 25-30 minutes
  • To New York Penn Station: 55-70 minutes (with transfer at Newark)
  • Monthly Pass: Approximately $380-$400
  • Peak Trains: Regular service during rush hours with express options

The Berkeley Heights train station offers convenient parking (permit required) and is located near the town center. Many residents appreciate the quieter, less crowded nature of the Raritan Valley Line compared to busier commuter routes.

Bus Service

  • NJ Transit Bus 113: Direct service to Port Authority Bus Terminal in approximately 50-65 minutes depending on traffic
  • Suburban Transit: Additional bus options to Manhattan

Driving

  • To Midtown Manhattan: 28-32 miles (45-75 minutes depending on traffic)
  • To Downtown Newark: 15 miles (20-30 minutes)
  • To Newark Liberty Airport: 12 miles (20-25 minutes)
  • Major Highways: I-78 (2 miles), I-287 (3 miles), Route 22 (adjacent), Garden State Parkway (15 minutes)

Berkeley Heights Real Estate FAQs

What is the average home price in Berkeley Heights NJ?

The median home price in Berkeley Heights ranges from $875,000 to $925,000 as of 2026. Entry-level homes start around $700,000, while larger updated properties and new construction can reach $1.4M+.

Are Berkeley Heights schools good?

Yes, Berkeley Heights Public Schools are consistently rated A+ and rank among the top districts in Union County. Governor Livingston High School is particularly well-regarded, ranking among New Jersey's top 100 public high schools.

How long is the commute from Berkeley Heights to NYC?

The train from Berkeley Heights to New York Penn Station takes 55-70 minutes with a transfer at Newark. Driving to Manhattan takes 45-75 minutes depending on traffic. Some residents drive to Summit (10 minutes) to catch the Midtown Direct train, which reaches Penn Station in 38-45 minutes.

What are property taxes like in Berkeley Heights?

Berkeley Heights has an effective tax rate of approximately 2.2%, which is reasonable for Union County. Annual taxes on a $875,000 home would be approximately $19,250.

What types of homes are available in Berkeley Heights?

Berkeley Heights primarily features colonial and split-level homes built between the 1950s-1980s. Many homes have been updated or expanded. Lot sizes are typically generous (0.25-0.5+ acres), which is a major selling point.

Is Berkeley Heights a good place to raise a family?

Absolutely. Berkeley Heights is known for its excellent schools, safe neighborhoods, active youth sports programs, and strong community spirit. The town consistently ranks among the best places to raise a family in New Jersey.

How competitive is the Berkeley Heights real estate market?

The market is competitive, with well-priced homes often receiving multiple offers. Average days on market is 17-35 days. Working with an experienced local agent who understands pricing strategy is essential.

What is the most desirable neighborhood in Berkeley Heights?

The "most desirable" area depends on your priorities. The East Side near McVey Avenue offers walkability to schools, while the West Side provides larger lots and newer homes. The Gloria Drive area is popular with young families.

Are there any condos or townhomes in Berkeley Heights?

Condo and townhome options are limited but available, typically priced between $450,000-$650,000. These properties are popular with empty nesters and first-time buyers.

What should I know about flooding in Berkeley Heights?

While Berkeley Heights is generally not in a high-risk flood zone, some properties near the Passaic River or its tributaries may require flood insurance. I can help you verify flood zone status for any property you're considering.

Are there HOA fees in Berkeley Heights?

Most Berkeley Heights neighborhoods do not have HOAs. However, some newer developments and condo communities do have monthly fees. I'll provide complete HOA information for any property you're considering.

What is the best time of year to sell in Berkeley Heights?

The spring market (March-May) and fall market (September-November) are typically strongest. However, Berkeley Heights' excellent schools mean families are looking year-round, particularly during winter for summer moves.

How much should I budget for utilities?

Typical monthly utility costs (electric, gas, water) range from $250-$400 for a single-family home, depending on size and season. Berkeley Heights has town-provided water and sewer services.

Is there public transportation besides the train?

Yes, NJ Transit Bus 113 provides direct service to Port Authority Bus Terminal. Many residents also drive to nearby Summit for the Midtown Direct train service.

What parks and recreation are available?

Berkeley Heights offers excellent recreation including the Watchung Reservation (2,000 acres), community pool, multiple local parks, and active youth sports leagues.

Are pets allowed everywhere in Berkeley Heights?

Single-family homes have no restrictions. Some condo and townhome communities may have pet restrictions. I can provide specific information for any property.

What is the racial and ethnic diversity like?

Berkeley Heights has become increasingly diverse in recent years, with a significant Asian-American population (approximately 20%) and growing representation from various backgrounds.

Can I have a home office in Berkeley Heights?

Absolutely. Many Berkeley Heights homes feature dedicated office space, finished basements that can serve as workspaces, or sufficient space to create a home office. The township allows home-based businesses with proper permits.
