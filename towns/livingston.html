Livingston NJ Real Estate Market Overview 2026

Livingston is one of Essex County's most sought-after communities, offering exceptional schools, beautiful homes, and convenient access to New York City. The town consistently ranks among New Jersey's best places to live, attracting families who prioritize education, safety, and quality of life.

Current Market Statistics

  • Median Home Price: $780,000 – $1,050,000
  • Average Price per Sq Ft: $385 – $485
  • Average Days on Market: 28 – 42 days
  • Inventory Level: 2.8 months (seller's market)
  • Year-Over-Year Price Change: +4.2% to +6.8%
  • Homes Selling Over Asking: 48%
  • Average Home Size: 2,800 – 3,500 sq ft

The Livingston real estate market remains highly competitive, particularly for move-in ready homes in the most desirable neighborhoods. Properties that are well-priced and professionally marketed often receive multiple offers within the first week. The luxury segment ($1M+) requires strategic positioning to attract qualified buyers from Manhattan and other NYC boroughs.

Market Trends for 2026

Buyers in Livingston are prioritizing updated kitchens with premium appliances, open-concept living spaces, home offices, and outdoor entertaining areas. Homes with finished basements and modern bathrooms command significant premiums. Properties near the Reservoir or with proximity to top schools see the strongest demand.

Livingston Neighborhoods Guide

East Livingston

Character: Newer construction, larger lots, family-oriented

Price Range: $850,000 – $1,400,000

Best For: Families seeking modern homes with spacious yards

East Livingston features some of the town's newer construction, with many homes built in the 1980s and 1990s. You'll find expanded colonials, center-hall designs, and some contemporary properties on generous lots ranging from 0.3 to 0.75 acres. The neighborhoods are quiet, tree-lined, and filled with families. Many homes feature updated kitchens, finished basements, and in-ground pools.

West Livingston

Character: Established neighborhoods, mature trees, traditional appeal

Price Range: $720,000 – $1,100,000

Best For: Buyers seeking character homes with established landscaping

West Livingston offers a more traditional suburban feel with homes primarily built between the 1950s and 1970s. The mature tree canopy creates a picturesque setting, and many streets have a genuine "neighborhood" feel where residents know each other. Home styles range from classic capes and ranches to split-levels and colonials.

South Livingston

Character: Convenient location, diverse housing stock, close to shopping

Price Range: $650,000 – $950,000

Best For: Commuters and those wanting proximity to Route 10 amenities

South Livingston offers convenient access to Route 10's extensive shopping corridor while maintaining a residential atmosphere. This area features a mix of housing styles including colonials, split-levels, and some newer townhomes.

The Reservoir Area

Character: Scenic views, walking trails, prestigious addresses

Price Range: $900,000 – $1,500,000+

Best For: Nature lovers and those seeking premium locations

The area surrounding the Livingston Reservoir represents some of the town's most prestigious real estate. Homes here often feature scenic views, larger lots, and access to walking trails around the water.

North Livingston

Character: Quiet streets, strong community, close to schools

Price Range: $750,000 – $1,200,000

Best For: Families prioritizing school proximity and neighborhood community

North Livingston offers a quintessential suburban experience with quiet, winding streets and a strong sense of community. Many homes are within walking distance of elementary schools and parks.

Livingston Town Center

Character: Walkable, convenient, mixed residential

Price Range: $550,000 – $850,000 (townhomes/condos)

Best For: Empty nesters, young professionals, and convenience seekers

The area around Livingston Town Center offers condominium and townhome options for those seeking a lower-maintenance lifestyle.
