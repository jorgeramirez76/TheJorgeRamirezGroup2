Livingston NJ Real Estate Market Overview 2026

Livingston is one of Essex County's most sought-after communities, offering a rare combination of excellent public schools, spacious suburban lots, and convenient access to major highways and NYC. Located approximately 22 miles west of Manhattan, Livingston attracts families who prioritize education and space.

Current Market Statistics

  • Median Home Price: $800,000 – $950,000
  • Average Price per Sq Ft: $360 – $450
  • Average Days on Market: 25 – 45 days
  • Homes Selling Over Asking: 40-50%
  • Year-Over-Year Price Change: +2.5% to +4.0%
  • Inventory Level: 2.4 months (seller's market)

The Livingston real estate market is consistently strong, supported by demand from families seeking excellent schools. Well-priced homes in move-in condition often receive multiple offers. The market tends to be particularly active in spring and early summer as families look to move before the school year.

Market Trends for 2026

Buyers in Livingston are prioritizing updated kitchens and bathrooms, open floor plans, finished basements, and level backyards. Homes in the most desirable school zones command premiums. The demand for home offices continues, with many buyers seeking dedicated workspace.

Home Styles & Housing Stock in Livingston

Livingston offers diverse architectural styles, with most homes built between the 1950s and 1980s:

  • Colonial Homes: The most prevalent style, typically featuring 4 bedrooms, 2.5 baths, and ranging from 2,400 to 3,600 square feet on lots of 0.3 to 0.5 acres.
  • Split-Level Homes: Popular in neighborhoods developed during the 1960s-1970s, offering flexible living spaces and typically priced between $750K-$900K.
  • Ranch Homes: Found in older sections, popular with empty nesters and those seeking single-level living or renovation opportunities.
  • Contemporary & Expanded Colonials: Many original homes have been significantly expanded and updated with modern amenities.
  • New Construction: Limited but available, typically tear-downs rebuilt with luxury finishes ($1.2M-$2M+).
  • Mid-Century Modern: Some distinctive homes from the 1950s-60s with architectural character.

Typical Lot Sizes: Livingston lots are generally generous, ranging from 0.3-0.6 acres, with some larger properties (0.75-1+ acres) near the reservoir and on the west side.

Neighborhoods in Livingston

Price Range: $750,000 – $1,000,000

Best For: Buyers seeking convenience to shopping and highways

This area features homes built primarily in the 1970s-1990s, many with updates and modern amenities. The proximity to Route 10 shopping (Willowbrook Mall area), restaurants, and major highways makes this popular with commuters. Homes here often feature open floor plans and two-car garages.

West Livingston / Summit Lane Area

Character: Established neighborhood with larger lots

Price Range: $850,000 – $1,200,000

Best For: Families seeking space, quiet streets, and prestige

West Livingston offers some of the township's most impressive properties on generous lots with mature trees. This area features a mix of expanded colonials, custom homes, and newer construction. The neighborhood offers a more suburban feel while still providing easy access to schools and shopping.

Reservoir Area / Northfield Avenue Corridor

Schools: Rankings & Performance

The Livingston Public School District is consistently ranked among the top districts in Essex County and is a primary driver of the local real estate market.

  • Collins Elementary School: A rating; serves northern Livingston with strong academic programs.
  • Mount Pleasant Elementary School: A rating; serves central areas with excellent special education resources.
  • Riker Hill Elementary School: A rating; serves southern areas with innovative curriculum.
  • Harrison Elementary School: A rating; known for its STEM focus and enrichment programs.

Middle School (6-8)

  • Heritage Middle School: A rating; consistently ranks among Essex County's top middle schools with strong academics, athletics, and performing arts.

High School (9-12)

NYC Commute & Transportation

Livingston is primarily a driving community, but multiple commuting options serve residents:

Bus Service

  • Coach USA Bus 77: Direct service to Port Authority Bus Terminal (45-60 minutes)
  • NJ Transit Bus: Multiple routes connecting to Newark and NYC
  • Monthly Pass: Approximately $300-$400

Commuter Rail Options

While Livingston doesn't have its own train station, nearby options include:

  • Short Hills Station (NJ Transit): 15 minute drive; direct Midtown Direct service
  • Millburn Station: 15 minute drive; Midtown Direct service
  • Summit Station: 20 minute drive; direct NYC service

Driving

  • To Midtown Manhattan: 25-28 miles (45-75 minutes depending on traffic)
  • To Downtown Newark: 12 miles (20-25 minutes)
  • To Newark Liberty Airport: 15 miles (20-25 minutes)
  • Major Highways: I-280 (adjacent), I-80 (5 minutes), Garden State Parkway (10 minutes), Route 10 (adjacent), Route 24 (15 minutes)

Livingston's highway access is exceptional, making it ideal for professionals who drive to work throughout Northern NJ or NYC.

Livingston Real Estate FAQs

What is the average home price in Livingston NJ?

The median home price in Livingston ranges from $800,000 to $950,000 as of 2026. Entry-level homes start around $700,000, while larger updated properties and new construction can reach $1.3M+.

Are Livingston schools good?

Yes, Livingston Public Schools are highly rated with an A+ rating at the high school and A ratings at elementary and middle schools. Livingston High School ranks among the top 50 in New Jersey.

Is there a train station in Livingston?

No, Livingston doesn't have its own train station. Residents typically drive to nearby Millburn, Short Hills, or Summit for NJ Transit service to NYC, or take the bus directly from Livingston.

What are property taxes in Livingston?

Livingston has an effective tax rate of approximately 2.8%, which is higher than some neighboring towns but reflects the quality of schools. Annual taxes on an $850,000 home would be approximately $23,800.
